# Boomp Drops editing rules

Use these rules when polishing the 100 drafted posts into publishable Boomp Drops pages and email source material.

## Main goal
Make each post:
- useful
- specific
- easy to read
- commercially sharp
- not obviously AI

## What to cut
Cut or rewrite anything that sounds like:
- generic marketing advice
- padded transitions
- explanation without tension
- symmetrical "first/second/third/fourth" lists unless the structure is truly necessary
- abstract claims about "visibility," "trust," or "consistency" unless they are grounded in a real customer behavior
- fake-serious phrasing like "what this means," "why this matters," or "in today's landscape" when the sentence does not earn it
- vague uplift language like "strong fit," "powerful," "important," or "helpful" unless it says exactly how
- obvious LLM rhythm where every paragraph has two calm sentences and a neat closer

## What to add
Add more of this instead:
- plainspoken claims
- lived business reality
- a little edge
- concrete buyer tension
- phrasing a real owner might actually say
- specific examples of what the owner is stuck doing
- sharper contrasts between what software promises and what the owner still carries

## Voice target
The voice should feel:
- calm
- smart
- slightly opinionated
- practical
- not salesy
- not "content marketer writing about content marketing"

The writing should sound like someone who actually understands:
- local businesses
- owner overload
- stale profiles
- trust leakage
- the difference between posting software and actual content help

## Structural rules
Each post should usually do this:
1. answer the question fast
2. name the real friction
3. explain the mismatch or false promise
4. show the better frame
5. land on a concrete next step or evaluation lens

## Style rules
- Prefer short headings in sentence case.
- Use "is," "are," and "has" when they are the clearest choice.
- Keep lists short unless a longer list truly helps.
- Break any paragraph that starts sounding assembled.
- Keep CTA language preview-first and specific.
- Do not overuse em dashes.
- Avoid overly polished closes. End with a real point, not a slogan.

## Glow/Boomp message guardrails
Keep coming back to the core truth:
- the problem is not posting mechanics
- the problem is the owner still carrying the content burden
- the better system starts from real business inputs
- the preview should feel close enough that the owner is reacting, not rewriting from scratch

## Editing tests
Before a post is done, ask:
1. Would a real owner nod at this or skim past it?
2. Is there any paragraph that could appear in 30 other AI-written marketing posts?
3. Are there any repeated sentence patterns that make the piece feel machine-made?
4. Did we say something specific enough to be worth indexing?
5. Does the CTA feel like the next logical move, not a tacked-on pitch?

If the answer is bad on any of those, keep editing.
