# Boomp Drops Operating Model

This content bank is the source system for **Boomp Drops**.

## What Boomp Drops is
Boomp Drops is not a separate content ideation project.

It is the **public + email packaging** of the existing website-first content bank:
- the **canonical page** lives on the new site and is ready for indexing
- the **email** is the weekly distribution layer
- the **social cuts** are pulled from the same core idea after the page and email exist

## Core decision
For this system, **one drafted canonical post = one Boomp Drop issue**.

That is the cleanest way to hit the real goal:
- two years of weekly Drops
- every Drop written ahead of time
- every Drop ready to become both a public indexed page and an email send

## Why this model wins
It keeps the work contained in one place and avoids splitting strategy across separate folders or duplicate workflows.

Instead of maintaining one system for blog posts and another for the newsletter, this structure makes the website page the source of truth and lets the newsletter adapt from it.

That gives Boomp Drops four jobs at once:
1. indexed search/AEO inventory
2. weekly email value
3. social source material
4. launch/rebrand proof that the new brand already has depth

## What “ready” means
A Drop is considered ready when all three layers are defined:

### 1. Canonical page
- markdown draft exists in `drafts/<cluster>/...`
- title, slug, description, CTA, and email angle are present
- post can be published/indexed on the new site

### 2. Email adaptation
- subject idea is clear
- hook is clear
- the point can be condensed into one email
- CTA points back to the canonical page or preview path

### 3. Distribution cuts
- 3–5 social cuts can be extracted after the page and email are finalized

## Weekly issue anatomy
Each Boomp Drop should contain:
- **subject**
- **hook**
- **one core insight**
- **one short why-it-matters-now section**
- **one CTA**
- **one canonical link target**

Optional add-ons:
- one “steal this angle” line
- one example line
- one preview CTA bridge

## Archive model
The archive should be organized around the same clusters used by the content bank:
- owner burden
- trust signals
- content sources
- buyers guides
- local visibility
- website engine
- objections
- proof and reviews
- seasonal operations
- AI search readiness

That way the archive is not just chronological. It also becomes a searchable and internally linkable library.

## Cadence
- Default cadence: **1 Boomp Drop per week**
- 100 drafted Drops = about **100 weeks** of publishing
- This gives roughly **two years** of content with room for a few reactive or seasonal swaps

## Launch sequencing rule
Publish the first wave in this order:
1. owner burden
2. trust signals
3. content-source system posts
4. buyers guides
5. local visibility
6. website-engine explanation posts
7. objections / proof / AI support

That order matches the strongest buyer-intent path:
- name the pain
- show the trust leak
- explain the mechanism
- help buyers compare options
- support local visibility behavior
- explain the page-first system

## Naming note
The content can still be referred to internally as **Boomp Drops** while the rebrand is in motion.

When the new brand name is finalized, the system can be renamed without changing the underlying structure because the operating model is already page-first and repo-contained.

## Bottom line
The goal is not to separately invent:
- a blog system
- a lead magnet
- a newsletter
- an archive

The goal is to maintain **one content engine** that can publish as all four.
