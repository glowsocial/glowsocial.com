# Content Taxonomy
## Cluster model
### Owner burden and hidden work
- **slug:** `owner-burden`
- **purpose:** Pain-first posts for owners who do not want to become content creators.

### Trust signals and active presence
- **slug:** `trust-signals`
- **purpose:** Posts about the online evidence customers check before they call.

### Content source systems
- **slug:** `content-sources`
- **purpose:** Posts that show how to turn existing business assets into content.

### Buyer decision and pricing guides
- **slug:** `buyers-guides`
- **purpose:** Posts that help owners compare DIY, freelancers, agencies, and systems.

### Local visibility and channel strategy
- **slug:** `local-visibility`
- **purpose:** Posts about Google Business Profile, Facebook, websites, and local discovery.

### Website-first content engine
- **slug:** `website-engine`
- **purpose:** Posts about making the website the canonical asset and adapting it into email/social.

### Objection handling
- **slug:** `objections`
- **purpose:** Posts that answer hesitation around time, quality, voice, and complexity.

### Proof, reviews, and examples
- **slug:** `proof-and-reviews`
- **purpose:** Posts that turn trust assets into repeatable content.

### Seasonal and operations content
- **slug:** `seasonal-operations`
- **purpose:** Posts tied to recurring customer questions, reminders, and service cycles.

### AI search and answer visibility
- **slug:** `ai-search-readiness`
- **purpose:** Posts about showing up in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and answer engines.

## Buyer-intent model
- **awareness:** explain the hidden problem or trust leak the owner may not have named yet
- **solution-aware:** explain why website-first / done-for-you baseline content solves the right problem
- **comparison:** compare tools, DIY, freelancers, agencies, or workflows by owner burden
- **objection:** reduce fear around voice, photos, AI quality, control, or setup effort
- **AEO / SEO:** answer plain-language search questions with durable pages that can be adapted into email/social later

## CTA rules
- Default CTA for high-intent Glow content: **see posts from your website first**
- Mid-funnel content can use softer bridge CTAs like: **preview the baseline**, **compare the workload**, or **see how website-first content works**
- Avoid generic “book a demo” as the first ask when the page is really about reducing buyer uncertainty

## Publishing principles
1. Start with **buyer pain and trust leakage**, not abstract marketing theory
2. Keep the website page as the **canonical asset**
3. Adapt outward into email/social instead of creating each channel from scratch
4. Prefer **plain-language direct answers** high on the page
5. Use internal links to build clusters around owner burden, trust, DIY-vs-help, and content-source systems
