Hootsuite has been the default answer to "how do I manage social media?" for over a decade. It's a powerful platform with a huge feature set — but for most local businesses, that power is the problem, not the solution.

## What Hootsuite Does

Hootsuite is a **social media management dashboard**. It lets you:

- Schedule posts across multiple platforms from one place
- Manage comments and messages in a unified inbox
- Track analytics and generate reports
- Manage team access and approvals
- Monitor brand mentions and keywords

It's genuinely useful — if you already have content to post.

## What Hootsuite Doesn't Do

This is the part that surprises most small business owners: **Hootsuite doesn't create any content for you.**

You still have to write every caption. Design every image. Build every calendar. Hootsuite just puts what you've already made onto a posting schedule. For a business with a marketing team producing content at volume, that's valuable. For a solo owner or small team with no dedicated content creator, you've paid $99+/month for a calendar that solves the wrong problem.

## Who Should Use Hootsuite

Hootsuite makes sense if you:
- Have a content team already producing posts at volume
- Need enterprise-level analytics across multiple brands
- Manage paid social ads alongside organic content
- Have dedicated social media staff who need a workflow tool

## Who Should Use Glow Social

Glow Social makes sense if you:
