Buffer is one of the simplest social media scheduling tools for small businesses. The pricing looks easy at first: free for a few channels, then pay per channel when you need more.

The part that confuses people is the limit structure. Buffer's free plan limit is not a monthly posting limit. It is a queue limit.

Here is the plain-English breakdown.

## Direct Answer: Buffer Free Plan Limits and Pricing

As of May 2026, Buffer's free plan lets you connect up to **3 channels** and keep up to **10 scheduled posts per channel** in the queue at one time. If you connect 3 channels, that means up to 30 scheduled posts waiting across the account.

Buffer's paid publishing plans are priced per channel:

- **Free:** $0, up to 3 channels, 10 scheduled posts per channel
- **Essentials:** $5 per channel/month billed yearly, or $6 per channel on monthly billing
- **Team:** $10 per channel/month billed yearly, or $12 per channel on monthly billing

Buffer is a strong choice when you already have posts ready and only need help scheduling them. It is not the best fit if your real bottleneck is creating content in the first place.

The important detail is that Buffer charges by channel. A Facebook Page is one channel. An Instagram profile is another. A Google Business Profile is another.

So if you want paid Buffer for Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Google Business Profile, the yearly Essentials math is:

**4 channels x $5 = $20/month billed yearly**

That is still inexpensive. But it only covers scheduling, not the work of making the posts.

## What Does "10 Scheduled Posts Per Channel" Mean?

On Buffer's free plan, you can have 10 posts waiting in the queue for each connected channel.

Example:

- Facebook Page: 10 scheduled posts
- Instagram profile: 10 scheduled posts
- LinkedIn Page: 10 scheduled posts

That is 30 scheduled posts total across the free account. If one Facebook post publishes tomorrow, the Facebook queue drops to 9 and you can schedule another Facebook post.

This is why Buffer's free plan can work well for light posting. If you post 2-3 times per week, 10 queued posts gives you a few weeks of runway.

Yes, Buffer has a real free plan. It is not just a short trial. The catch is that the free plan works best when:

- You have 3 or fewer channels
- You post lightly
- You do not need advanced analytics

- You are willing to create every post yourself

## The Hidden Cost: Your Time

Buffer can be free, but social media still takes time.

For a typical local business, the work looks like this:

- Decide what to post
- Write the caption
- Find or create an image
- Resize or format the visual
- Adjust the caption for each platform
- Add hashtags or local details
- Schedule it in Buffer
- Check comments and performance

Even with a good scheduler, that is usually 3-5 hours per month.

If your time is worth $25/hour, a free Buffer account can still have a true cost of $75-125/month in owner time.

That is why free scheduling tools are great for people who enjoy content creation, but frustrating for owners who simply want social media handled.

## When Buffer Is Worth It

Choose Buffer if:

- You already create your own content
- You want a clean, affordable scheduler
- You manage a small number of channels
- You need Google Business Profile, Bluesky, Mastodon, or broad platform support
- You prefer simple tools over heavy dashboards

Buffer is especially good when your problem is distribution: you have posts, but you need a better way to publish them.

## When Buffer Is Not Enough

Buffer is not enough if:

- You do not know what to post
- You do not have images ready
- You keep forgetting to fill the calendar
- You need posts written in your business voice
- You want a consistent monthly presence without managing a tool

In that case, a scheduler does not solve the root problem. It gives you a blank calendar with better buttons.
