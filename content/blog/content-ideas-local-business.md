Struggling for social media content ideas? Here are 40 post ideas that work for almost any local business—restaurants, salons, contractors, retail, professional services. Mix these throughout your month for a balanced content mix.


## Educational Content (Tips and How-Tos)

<ol>
<li>A quick tip related to your industry that saves time or money</li>
<li>Common mistake customers make (and how to avoid it)</li>
<li>FAQ answered in detail—pick your most common question</li>
<li>"How to choose the right [product/service]" guidance</li>
<li>Seasonal tip relevant to your business (spring cleaning, winter prep, etc.)</li>
<li>Myth vs. reality in your industry</li>
<li>Simple tutorial showing your expertise</li>
<li>"What to expect when..." explaining your process</li>
</ol>

## Behind-the-Scenes Content

<ol start="9">
<li>Day in the life at your business</li>
<li>How you prepare for [service/product]</li>
<li>Your workspace, shop, or office</li>
<li>Tools, equipment, or materials you use (and why you chose them)</li>
<li>Before a project starts vs. in progress vs. finished</li>
<li>Packing orders, prepping for service, setup</li>
<li>Your morning or opening routine</li>
<li>End of day wrap-up</li>
</ol>

## Team and Personal Content

<ol start="17">
<li>Introduce a team member</li>
<li>Staff appreciation post</li>
<li>Why you started this business</li>
<li>Your professional background or training</li>
<li>What you love about your work</li>
<li>Something your team is celebrating</li>
<li>Fun fact about you or your team</li>
<li>Team outing, celebration, or milestone</li>
</ol>

## Customer and Community Content

<ol start="25">
<li>Customer testimonial or review (with permission)</li>
<li>Thank you post to a specific customer (with permission)</li>
<li>Before/after transformation or project result</li>
<li>Customer success story</li>
<li>How long you've been serving the community</li>
<li>Local event you're attending or sponsoring</li>
<li>Shoutout to another local business you love</li>
<li>Community involvement or charity work</li>
</ol>

## Promotional Content

<ol start="33">
<li>Highlight a specific service and who it's for</li>
<li>New service, product, or menu item announcement</li>
<li>Limited-time offer or seasonal promotion</li>
<li>Reminder about a service people forget you offer</li>
<li>Gift card or gift idea suggestion (especially before holidays)</li>
<li>"Now booking for [season/event]" reminder</li>
</ol>

## Engagement Content

<ol start="39">
<li>Ask a question related to your industry</li>
<li>This or that poll (simple preference question)</li>
</ol>

## How to Use These Ideas

<strong>Create a rotation:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Week 1: Educational + Behind-the-scenes</li>
<li>Week 2: Team/Personal + Customer story</li>
<li>Week 3: Educational + Community</li>
<li>Week 4: Promotional + Engagement</li>
</ul>

<strong>Batch create monthly:</strong>

<ol>
<li>Pick 12 ideas from this list</li>
<li>Write all captions in one session</li>
<li>Create or gather images</li>
<li>Schedule for the month</li>
</ol>

## Content Mix Guidelines

For a balanced month:


<ul>
<li>40% Educational/Helpful content</li>
<li>25% Behind-the-scenes/Personal content</li>
<li>20% Customer/Community content</li>
<li>15% Promotional content</li>
</ul>

This keeps your feed valuable without feeling like constant sales pitches.


## Making Content Faster

<strong>Photo tips:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Take photos during regular work, not in separate sessions</li>
<li>Batch capture: take 10 photos on one day and use throughout month</li>
<li>Phone photos are fine—authentic often outperforms polished</li>
</ul>

<strong>Caption tips:</strong>

<ul>
<li>2-3 sentences is often enough</li>
<li>Start with your main point (people don't read full captions)</li>
<li>Add a simple question or CTA at the end</li>
</ul>
