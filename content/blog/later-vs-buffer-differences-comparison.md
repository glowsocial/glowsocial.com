## Pricing Breakdown

Here's what each tool actually costs for a typical small business managing 3-4 social media platforms:

### Later Pricing (2026)
- **Free:** 1 social set (1 profile per platform), 5 posts per profile/month
- **Starter ($18/month):** 1 social set, 30 posts per profile, basic analytics
- **Growth ($40/month):** 3 social sets, 150 posts per profile, full analytics
- **Advanced ($80/month):** 6 social sets, unlimited posts

### Buffer Pricing (2026)
- **Free:** 3 channels, 10 scheduled posts per channel
- **Essentials ($6/month per channel):** Unlimited posts, engagement tools, landing page
- **Team ($12/month per channel):** Collaboration, approval workflows, unlimited team members

## Where Later Wins

### 1. Visual Content Calendar
Later's drag-and-drop visual calendar is genuinely better than Buffer's. You can see your entire content plan laid out visually, preview how your Instagram grid will look before posting, and rearrange posts by dragging them. If your content is image-heavy, this matters.

### 2. Instagram Grid Preview
Later lets you preview exactly how your Instagram feed will look before publishing. For brands where visual consistency matters (photographers, restaurants, retail, beauty), this feature alone can justify the price difference.

### 3. Linkin.bio
Later's link-in-bio tool creates a shoppable landing page from your Instagram posts. It's more mature than Buffer's Start Page for Instagram-focused businesses.

### 4. Media Library
Later's media library is built for visual content management. Upload, organize, and tag images for easy reuse. Buffer's media management is more basic.

## Where Buffer Wins

### 1. Simpler Pricing
Buffer's per-channel pricing is more transparent and usually cheaper for multi-platform users. You pay for what you use, and there are no tiers to navigate.

### 2. More Platform Support
Buffer supports Mastodon and Bluesky in addition to the major platforms. If you're active on emerging social networks, Buffer has broader reach.

### 3. Cleaner Interface
Buffer's interface is intentionally minimal. There's less to learn, fewer menus, and a faster path from "I have a post" to "it's scheduled." For people who find social media tools overwhelming, Buffer is less intimidating.

### 4. AI Assistant
Buffer's AI assistant can generate caption suggestions, repurpose content across platforms, and adjust tone. It's not a content creation engine — you still guide it — but it's useful for writers who get stuck.

### 5. Start Page
Buffer's landing page builder (Start Page) works for any platform, not just Instagram. It's a simple way to create a link-in-bio page with custom links, branding, and design.

## The Honest Limitations of Both

Here's what neither Later nor Buffer will do for you:

- **Create content from scratch.** Both tools schedule posts. You still write the captions, design the graphics, take the photos, and plan the strategy.
- **Guarantee consistency.** The tool can schedule posts, but only if you create them first. If you stop creating content, your posting stops too.
- **Manage Google Business Profile.** Neither Later nor Buffer can post to GBP, which matters for local businesses.
- **Handle engagement automatically.** Neither responds to comments or DMs for you.

This is the fundamental distinction between *scheduling tools* and *done-for-you services*.
