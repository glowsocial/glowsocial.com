**How often should a small business post on social media?** 3-5 times per week across your active platforms. That's 12-20 posts per month. Post less than that and your pages look abandoned. Post more than once per day and you hit diminishing returns.

Here's the complete posting schedule — when to post, how to batch it, and how to automate it entirely.


## The Ideal Weekly Schedule

For most small businesses, this framework works:

<table>
<tr><th>Day</th><th>Content Type</th><th>Why</th></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Monday</strong></td><td>Educational tip</td><td>Start the week with value — positions you as the expert</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Wednesday</strong></td><td>Behind-the-scenes / team</td><td>Midweek personal content builds connection</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Friday</strong></td><td>Social proof or promotion</td><td>End the week with credibility — reviews, results, offers</td></tr>
</table>

That's 3 posts per week — the minimum effective dose. If you want to do more:

<table>
<tr><th>Day</th><th>Content Type</th></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Tuesday</strong></td><td>Customer story or case study</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Thursday</strong></td><td>Engagement post (question, poll, "this or that")</td></tr>
</table>

5 posts per week is the ceiling for most small businesses. Beyond that, you're creating content for content's sake.


## Best Times to Post by Platform

These are general guidelines based on 2026 engagement data. Your audience may vary.

<table>
<tr><th>Platform</th><th>Best Days</th><th>Best Times</th><th>Post Frequency</th></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Facebook</strong></td><td>Tues-Thurs</td><td>9-11 AM, 1-3 PM</td><td>3-5x/week</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Instagram</strong></td><td>Tues-Fri</td><td>10 AM-12 PM, 7-9 PM</td><td>3-5x/week</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>LinkedIn</strong></td><td>Tues-Thurs</td><td>7-9 AM, 12 PM</td><td>2-3x/week</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>TikTok</strong></td><td>Mon-Fri</td><td>6-9 PM</td><td>3-7x/week</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Google Business Profile</strong></td><td>Any</td><td>Any</td><td>1-2x/week</td></tr>
</table>

**Important:** Timing matters less than consistency. A post at 2 PM on a Thursday will still reach your audience. An empty page at the "perfect time" reaches nobody.


## How Many Platforms Should You Be On?

**Start with 2-3 platforms.** For most small businesses:

<ul>
<li><strong>Facebook + Instagram</strong> — if your customers are consumers (B2C)</li>
<li><strong>LinkedIn + Facebook</strong> — if your customers are businesses (B2B)</li>
<li><strong>Instagram + TikTok</strong> — if your business is visual (salon, restaurant, photography)</li>
<li><strong>All of the above + Google Business Profile</strong> — if you use done-for-you software (no extra work)</li>
</ul>

The mistake most businesses make: signing up for 6 platforms and posting consistently on none. Two active platforms beat five abandoned ones.


## The 2-Hour Monthly Batch Method

If you're doing this yourself, here's how to create a month of content in one focused session:

**Hour 1: Plan + Write**
<ol>
<li>Pick 12 topics using the Mon/Wed/Fri framework above (10 min)</li>
<li>Write all 12 captions (40 min — don't overthink, aim for "good enough")</li>
<li>Review and edit (10 min)</li>
</ol>

**Hour 2: Design + Schedule**
<ol>
<li>Create or gather images for each post (30 min)</li>
<li>Load everything into your scheduler — Buffer, Later, or Meta Business Suite (20 min)</li>
<li>Double-check dates and platforms (10 min)</li>
</ol>

**Monthly time commitment: 2 hours.** Block it on your calendar for the 1st of each month. If you skip it, your pages go dark.

## What Happens If You Stop Posting?

The data is clear: businesses that post inconsistently perform worse than those that never posted at all. Here's why:

<ul>
<li><strong>Algorithmic penalty:</strong> Platforms deprioritize accounts that go dormant and come back</li>
<li><strong>Trust erosion:</strong> A customer sees your last post was 4 months ago and assumes you closed</li>
<li><strong>Competitor advantage:</strong> Every day you don't post, someone else in your market does</li>
</ul>

The best posting schedule is the one you'll actually maintain. If you can't sustain 3 posts per week yourself, automate it.
