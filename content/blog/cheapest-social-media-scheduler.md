But the cheapest subscription is not always the cheapest way to keep a business visible. A scheduler only saves money if you already have posts, captions, images, and a plan. If you are still doing all of that yourself, the true cost includes your time.

Here is the plain comparison.

## Best Free Option: Meta Business Suite

Meta Business Suite is the cheapest option if your social media presence is mostly Facebook and Instagram.

It lets you create and schedule content for Meta platforms without paying for a third-party scheduler. For a small business that only cares about Facebook and Instagram, that can be enough.

Use Meta Business Suite if:

- You only need Facebook and Instagram.
- You already have photos and captions ready.
- You do not need LinkedIn, TikTok, Google Business Profile, Pinterest, or broader analytics.
- You want to keep software costs at $0.

Skip it if you need a single calendar across several platforms, or if Google Business Profile matters for local visibility.

## Best Cheap Multi-Platform Scheduler: Buffer

Buffer is one of the clearest low-cost schedulers because the pricing is simple.

As of this June 2026 check, Buffer's pricing page listed:

- **Free:** up to 3 channels, with 10 scheduled posts per channel.
- **Essentials:** $5 per channel/month when billed yearly.
- **Team:** $10 per channel/month when billed yearly.

The important detail: Buffer charges by channel. A Facebook Page is one channel. An Instagram profile is another. A Google Business Profile is another.

So if you want paid scheduling for Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Google Business Profile, the basic annual-billing math is:

**4 channels x $5 = $20/month**

That is genuinely affordable. It is a good answer for someone who already has posts ready and wants a clean, simple scheduler.

The limitation is also simple: Buffer helps publish the work. It does not remove the work.

For the deeper breakdown, read [Buffer free plan limits and pricing](/blog/buffer-pricing-free-plan-limits-2026).

## Best Cheap Account-Based Scheduler: Publer

Publer is another strong low-cost option, especially if you like account-based pricing and simple queueing.

Publer's help center describes a free plan that can connect up to 3 social accounts, excluding X/Twitter, with queue limits. It also describes Professional pricing starting around $5/month for 1 social account, with additional account/member pricing.

Use Publer if:

- You want a low-cost tool with a real free plan.
- You manage a small number of social accounts.
- You want scheduling, drafts, media storage, and basic workflow features.
- You are comfortable checking the current pricing calculator before choosing.

The same warning applies: low subscription cost is only useful if you have content to schedule.

## Best Cheap Scheduler With Analytics: Metricool

Metricool is often a better fit when the buyer wants more than a calendar.

As of this June 2026 check, Metricool's pricing page showed a free plan for 1 brand with limited monthly scheduling, competitor analysis, and 30 days of analytics. Paid Starter pricing appears above the free tier and varies by billing/currency view.

Use Metricool if:

- You want scheduling plus analytics.
- You care about competitor tracking.
- You manage one brand and want a useful free starting point.
- You want more reporting than a very simple scheduler provides.

Metricool is still a DIY tool. It can show you what happened. It will not create the monthly content plan by itself.

For a related comparison, see [Metricool vs Hootsuite for small business](/blog/metricool-vs-hootsuite-for-small-business).

## Best Visual Scheduler: Later

Later is not usually the cheapest social media scheduler. It belongs in this list because a lot of people searching for cheap scheduling are comparing Later, Buffer, and similar tools.

As of this June 2026 check, Later's pricing page listed paid scheduling plans starting at $18.75/month when billed yearly for Starter. Later's help center also describes a limited Free plan for creators who need basic scheduling or Link in Bio access.

Later is worth considering if:

- Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, or visual planning matter a lot.
- You already create photos and videos.
- You want grid planning, visual calendar workflows, or creator-style tools.
- You are okay paying more than the absolute lowest-cost scheduler.

Later is less compelling if your business mainly needs a simple, cheap way to post across Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Google Business Profile.

## The Hidden Cost of the Cheapest Scheduler

A cheap scheduler is only cheap when the content is already handled.

Most small businesses still have to:

- Decide what to post.
- Write the caption.
- Find or take the photo.
- Resize the image or video.
- Adjust the post for each platform.
- Add links, hashtags, or local context.
- Refill the queue when it runs out.

If that takes 4 hours per month, the true cost changes fast:

## When a Cheap Scheduler Is Enough

A cheap scheduler is enough when the business already has a content habit.

Choose a scheduler if:

- You enjoy making posts.
- You already have photos, videos, or offers ready.
- You know what your audience needs to hear.
- You only need a better way to queue posts.
- You can spend a few hours each month filling the calendar.

In that case, start simple. Use Meta Business Suite if you only need Facebook and Instagram. Use Buffer or Publer if you want a broader free or low-cost scheduler. Use Metricool if analytics matter. Use Later if visual planning matters.

## When a Cheap Scheduler Is the Wrong Buy

A cheap scheduler is the wrong buy when the page goes quiet because nobody has time to create posts.

That usually sounds like:

- "We bought a tool, but we never fill it."
- "I do not know what to post."
- "The calendar looks organized for two weeks, then it dies."
- "I need more than Facebook and Instagram handled."
- "I do not want another dashboard."

That is not a scheduling problem. That is a content supply problem.
