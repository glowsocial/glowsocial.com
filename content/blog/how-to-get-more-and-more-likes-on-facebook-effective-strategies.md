Getting likes on Facebook isn't about going viral. For local businesses, it's about **showing up consistently** so the people who need your services actually see you.

Here's the problem: most local business owners try to "hack" the algorithm. They post a few times, see no engagement, and give up. But Facebook rewards consistency more than creativity.

## Why Facebook Likes Still Matter for Local Businesses

Likes are a trust signal. When someone searches for a plumber, a dentist, or a restaurant and finds your Facebook page with regular posts and decent engagement, it tells them you're active and legitimate.

An empty or abandoned page does the opposite — it makes people wonder if you're still in business.

## Strategies That Actually Work

### 1. Post Consistently (Even When Nobody Engages)

The algorithm needs data. If you post once a month, Facebook doesn't know what to do with your content. Post at least 3-4 times per week and the platform starts showing your content to more people.

### 2. Use Photos of Your Actual Work

Stock photos get scrolled past. Photos of your team, your jobs, your storefront — those stop the scroll. They're real, they're local, and they're what people want to see.

### 3. Ask Simple Questions

"What's your go-to weeknight dinner?" works better than a 500-word industry thought piece. Questions invite engagement, and engagement tells Facebook to show your posts to more people.

### 4. Post When Your Customers Are Online

For most local businesses, that's early morning (7-8 AM), lunch (12-1 PM), and evening (7-9 PM). Test different times and see what works for your audience.

### 5. Don't Forget Google Business Profile

Many customers find you on Google first. If your Google Business Profile is active, they'll follow the trail to your Facebook page. Make sure both are consistent.
