## Direct Answer

Freelance social media managers charge $300-$800 per month for small business accounts, typically delivering 8-12 posts per month with basic reporting. Full-time social media managers cost $35,000-$55,000 per year. AI alternatives like Glow Social cost $99 per month for comparable content output.

## Why This Matters

The cost spectrum is wider than most business owners realize. At the low end, a college student managing your Instagram for $200 per month will give you generic content with questionable strategy. At the high end, a seasoned agency charges $2,000-$5,000 per month for full-service management including content, community engagement, paid ads, and strategy. Most small businesses land in the middle ($300-$800 range) where they get a freelancer juggling 10-15 clients, providing templated content with monthly reports. The question isn't just "what does it cost?" but "what am I actually getting for that cost, and is there a cheaper way to get the same output?"

## Real-World Example

A boutique fitness studio interviewed three freelancers. Quotes ranged from $350 to $600 per month for 8-12 Instagram posts and a monthly performance report. The $350 freelancer delivered stock photos with generic fitness captions. The $600 freelancer took branded photos on-site but required 2 hours per month of the owner's time for content planning calls. Glow Social generated 12 fitness-specific posts for $99 per month with 10 minutes of setup and no ongoing time commitment.

## What Most People Get Wrong

The quoted price is never the total cost. A $400/month freelancer also requires your time for onboarding (2-4 weeks), weekly or monthly calls (30-60 minutes), content approval cycles, and the mental overhead of managing another vendor. Factor in the time cost and the true price is $500-$700 per month for most small business owners.
