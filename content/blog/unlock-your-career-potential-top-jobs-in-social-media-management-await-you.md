Social media management has become a real career. From freelance content creators to in-house marketing directors, the field offers multiple paths — and local businesses are the ones hiring.

## Social Media Management Career Paths

**Social Media Coordinator ($35-50K/year):** Entry-level role handling day-to-day posting, scheduling, and basic community management.

**Social Media Manager ($50-75K/year):** Creates strategy, manages content calendars, runs ads, and reports on analytics.

**Social Media Director ($75-120K/year):** Oversees the social media team, sets brand strategy, and manages budgets across platforms.

**Freelance Social Media Manager ($500-5,000/month per client):** Works independently with multiple businesses, handling their social media presence.

## What Local Businesses Actually Need

Most local businesses don't need a full-time social media manager. They need:

- Consistent posts (3-4x per week)
- Professional graphics
- Platform coverage (Facebook, Instagram, Google Business Profile)
- Content that reflects their services

This typically costs $300-500/month for a freelancer, or $2,000+/month for an agency.
