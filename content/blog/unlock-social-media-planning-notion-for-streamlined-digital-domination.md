Notion is a great tool for organizing just about anything, including social media content. But for local business owners, there's a gap between having a planning tool and actually using it consistently.

## How to Use Notion for Social Media Planning

If you want to try the DIY approach, here's a basic Notion setup:

1. **Create a content database** with columns for platform, content type, caption, image, and scheduled date
2. **Set up a calendar view** to see your posting schedule at a glance
3. **Create templates** for recurring content types (tips, promotions, behind-the-scenes)
4. **Add a kanban board** for tracking content from idea → draft → approved → published

This is a great system — if you have 5-10 hours per month to fill it with content.

## The Problem with Planning Tools

Here's what happens: you set up a beautiful Notion board, fill it with ideas during a motivated Saturday morning, and then never touch it again because the actual work — writing, designing graphics, formatting for each platform — takes more time than you have.

Planning tools help you organize work. They don't do the work.
