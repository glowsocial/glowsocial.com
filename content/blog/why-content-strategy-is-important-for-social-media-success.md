A content strategy turns random social media posting into a system that builds your business. Without one, you're throwing spaghetti at the wall.

## What Happens Without a Content Strategy

- You post when you feel like it (usually not often enough)
- Your content has no theme or direction
- You can't measure what's working because there's nothing to measure against
- You burn out and stop posting for weeks or months
- Your social media pages look abandoned

## The Four Pillars of a Local Business Content Strategy

### 1. Content Categories
Define 4-5 types of content you'll rotate through:
- **Educational** — tips, how-tos, industry advice
- **Social proof** — reviews, testimonials, case studies
- **Behind the scenes** — your team, your process, your workspace
- **Promotional** — offers, new services, pricing
- **Community** — local events, partnerships, shout-outs to other businesses

### 2. Posting Schedule
Pick days and times that work. Three posts per week is a solid minimum. Same days, same times — consistency is more important than frequency.

### 3. Platform Focus
Don't try to be everywhere. For most local businesses: Facebook, Instagram, and Google Business Profile cover 90% of your audience.

### 4. Review and Adjust
Once a month, look at what got engagement and what didn't. Do more of what works. Drop what doesn't.
