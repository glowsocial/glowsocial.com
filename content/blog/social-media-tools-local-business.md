The best social media tools for local businesses solve a specific problem: staying visible online without spending hours you don't have. For most local businesses, that means either <strong>DIY scheduling tools</strong> (Buffer, Later) or <strong>automated posting software</strong> like Glow Social that creates and publishes content for $99/month.


This guide covers what actually works for restaurants, salons, contractors, and other local businesses—not enterprise tools designed for marketing teams.


## What Local Businesses Actually Need

Before comparing tools, understand what matters for local social media:


<ul>
<li><strong>Consistency over perfection:</strong> Posting regularly matters more than viral content</li>
<li><strong>Platform focus:</strong> Facebook and Instagram reach local customers; LinkedIn and TikTok depend on your business type</li>
<li><strong>Time efficiency:</strong> Most local business owners have maybe 1-2 hours per week for marketing—if that</li>
<li><strong>Local relevance:</strong> Content should connect to your community, not feel generic</li>
</ul>

## DIY vs Automated: Which Approach Works for You?

<strong>Choose DIY tools (Buffer, Later, Canva) if:</strong>

<ul>
<li>You have 3-5 hours per month for social media</li>
<li>You enjoy creating content or have staff to help</li>
<li>You want complete control over every post</li>
<li>You're comfortable learning new software</li>
</ul>

<strong>Choose automated posting (Glow Social) if:</strong>

<ul>
<li>You don't have time to create content regularly</li>
<li>You've tried posting consistently but it always falls off</li>
<li>You want visibility without the ongoing work</li>
<li>You'd rather focus on serving customers than managing social media</li>
</ul>
