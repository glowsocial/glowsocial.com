LinkedIn Company Pages are the most underused platform for local and professional service businesses. Everyone has a personal profile — almost nobody has a properly set up company page.

Here's how to create one that works.

## Who Needs a LinkedIn Company Page?

Every business, but especially:

- **B2B companies** — LinkedIn is where your buyers research vendors
- **Professional services** — lawyers, accountants, consultants, financial advisors
- **Coaches and consultants** — establish credibility beyond your personal brand
- **Real estate agents** — attract commercial clients and referral partners
- **Anyone hiring** — candidates look at your Company Page before applying

## Step 1: Create the Page

1. Go to [linkedin.com/company/setup/new](https://www.linkedin.com/company/setup/new/)
2. Choose **Company** (not Showcase Page)
3. Enter your company name, URL, industry, and size
4. Confirm you're authorized to represent this organization
5. Click **Create page**

## Step 2: Add Your Visual Assets

**Logo:** 300x300 pixels. Same logo you use everywhere else — consistency matters.

**Cover image:** 1128x191 pixels. Use a branded graphic, a team photo, or a shot of your work. This is prime real estate — don't leave it blank.

## Step 3: Write Your About Section (This Is Where Most People Fail)

LinkedIn gives you **2,000 characters** for your About section. Most companies write two sentences and call it done. Don't do that.

LinkedIn's algorithm indexes your About section for search. More text = more keywords = more discoverability.

### What to include:

**Paragraph 1 — What you do and who you serve:**
Lead with the problem you solve, not your company history. "Small businesses struggle to maintain a consistent social media presence" beats "Founded in 2024, Glow Social is..."

**Paragraph 2 — How you're different:**
What's your approach? What do you believe that your competitors don't? What would a customer say about working with you?

**Paragraph 3 — Your services:**
List your key services with brief descriptions. LinkedIn indexes these for search.

**Paragraph 4 — Location and contact:**
Where you operate, how to get in touch, and a call to action.

## Step 4: Add Company Details

Fill out every field LinkedIn gives you:
- **Website URL**
- **Industry**
- **Company size**
- **Founded year**
- **Specialties** (up to 20 keywords — use them all)

The Specialties field is a hidden SEO gem. Add keywords like "social media management," "content creation," "local business marketing," etc.

## Step 5: Publish Your First Update

Post something immediately after creating your page. An empty page with zero posts looks abandoned.

Simple first post: introduce your company, what you do, and invite people to follow.
