# How to start social media marketing with no budget

To start social media marketing with no budget, set up free business profiles on Google Business Profile, Facebook, and Instagram using your existing website content. Use a [free setup tool](/setup) to generate platform-specific bios, pull your logo, and identify which accounts you still need to create — then post 3 times per week using your phone and your existing expertise.

## Why This Matters
The barrier to social media isn't money — it's time and clarity. Creating accounts is free on every platform. The cost comes from figuring out what to write in your bio, what size your images should be, which platforms matter for your industry, and what to actually post. A [social media starter kit](/blog/social-media-starter-kit-small-business) eliminates the research phase so you can go from "I should probably do this" to "I'm set up on every platform" in a single afternoon — without spending a dollar.

## Real-World Example
A new dog walker launched her business with literally zero marketing budget. She used a free profile setup tool to create optimized bios for Google Business Profile, Facebook, Instagram, and Nextdoor in one sitting. She then posted one photo per day from her walks — nothing fancy, just her phone camera and a caption about the dog's personality. Within three months, she had 15 recurring clients, entirely from organic social media visibility and Google Maps discovery. Her total marketing spend: $0.

## What Most People Get Wrong
Waiting until they have a budget before starting social media. The profiles are free. The content is your expertise. The images are on your phone. Every day you delay is a day your competitors' profiles are the ones customers find instead of yours. Start with the [free basics](/blog/social-media-checklist-new-business) — bios, profiles, and 3 posts per week — and invest in automation or help only after you've proven the channel works for your business.
