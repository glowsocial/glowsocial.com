Metricool and Hootsuite both help manage social media, but they are built for different buying moments.

## Quick Answer

For most small businesses, **Metricool is the better fit when you already create your own content and want affordable scheduling plus analytics**. Choose **Hootsuite** when you need a heavier team dashboard with inbox, listening, collaboration, and broader reporting.

Choose **neither** if the real problem is that you do not have posts ready. Metricool and Hootsuite can organize, schedule, and measure social media work, but they still leave the business owner responsible for deciding what to post, writing captions, creating visuals, and refilling the calendar next month.

Metricool is usually the lighter, lower-cost analytics and scheduling tool. Hootsuite is the heavier management dashboard with inbox, listening, collaboration, and broader reporting.

For small businesses, the real question is not "Which has more features?" It is "Which one removes the problem we actually have?"

Choose **Hootsuite** when you need a larger social media command center: inbox, listening, analytics, collaboration, paid/organic reporting, and enough budget to justify a heavier platform.

Choose **neither** if the problem is that you do not have posts ready. In that case, a dashboard will organize the work, but it will not make the work disappear.

## Metricool: Best for Lower-Cost Scheduling and Analytics

Metricool is usually the better small-business choice when the business wants data without buying a large enterprise-style dashboard.

As of this June 2026 refresh, Metricool's public pricing showed:

- **Free:** $0/month, 1 brand.
- **Free plan limits:** scheduling up to 20 posts per month, basic analytics history, competitor analysis, planner tools, and limited AI credits.
- **Starter:** paid plans with brand-count tiers, including up to 5 brands from $20/month in the annual USD view.
- **Advanced:** higher tiers for more brands, team/client management, approvals, reporting templates, and deeper analytics features.

Metricool is a good fit if:

- You want scheduling plus analytics.
- You manage one brand or a small number of brands.
- You want competitor tracking without a large software bill.
- You create your own posts and need a better calendar/reporting layer.
- You care about measuring what is working.

Metricool is not a good fit if you expect the tool to create your monthly content plan for you.

## Hootsuite: Best for a Heavier Social Dashboard

Hootsuite is better when the team needs a full social media management system.

Hootsuite's Standard plan page describes:

- Managing up to 10 social media accounts.
- Unlimited post scheduling.
- A centralized inbox for public and private messages.
- Analytics, best-time recommendations, and report exports.
- Competitive benchmarking.
- Social listening for mentions, topics, trends, and sentiment.
- AI-assisted captions, hashtags, content ideas, and trend-based posts.
- Collaboration tools when more than one user is involved.

That is more tool than many local businesses need. It can be useful for a small team with an active marketing function, but it is often too much for a business owner who only needs a consistent baseline presence.

Hootsuite is a good fit if:

- You manage many accounts.
- Messages and comments need a real inbox workflow.
- You care about listening, sentiment, or trends.
- You need multiple users, reporting, and collaboration.
- Someone on the team already owns content creation.

Hootsuite is not the first tool to buy if the calendar is empty because nobody has time to make posts.

## Metricool vs Hootsuite for Local Businesses

For a local business, Metricool usually wins when the owner or marketer wants:

- A cleaner calendar.
- Basic analytics.
- Competitor tracking.
- Lower software cost.
- A free starting point.

Hootsuite usually wins when the business has:

- A team managing social.
- High message volume.
- A need for listening and brand monitoring.
- Multiple users or approvals.
- A broader reporting requirement.

For a 2-person service business, Hootsuite can be more dashboard than necessary. For a multi-location brand or a team with active inbound messages, Hootsuite may be worth the added complexity.

## The Part Neither Tool Solves

Metricool and Hootsuite both assume someone has content to manage.

That means someone still has to:

- Decide what to post.
- Write captions.
- Choose images or video.
- Adapt the post for each platform.
- Review performance.
- Refill the calendar next month.

This is the content-creation gap. Most small businesses do not fail because they picked the wrong dashboard. They fail because the dashboard goes empty.
