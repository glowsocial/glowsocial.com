# Glow Social vs hiring a social media manager: which is better?

## Quick answer

Glow Social is usually better when a small business mainly needs consistent posts created, reviewed, and published without managing another person. A social media manager is better when the business needs strategy, comments and DMs handled, campaigns, video production, reporting, paid ads, or reputation-sensitive judgment.

For many local businesses, the first problem is not that they lack a social media department. It is that Facebook, Instagram, and Google Business Profile go quiet because the owner has to invent every post. Glow Social is built for that baseline: prepare review-ready posts from the business context so the owner can approve them instead of starting from a blank page.

## The practical comparison

| Decision point | Glow Social | Hiring a social media manager |
|---|---|---|
| Typical monthly cost | Starts at $99/month | Commonly $300-$1,500+/month depending on scope |
| Best for | Recurring post creation and publishing consistency | Strategy, judgment, engagement, campaigns, and custom creative work |
| Owner workload | Review, approve, and occasionally respond to comments | Manage the person, give context, approve work, and review performance |
| Setup speed | Fast when the website already explains the business | Slower because a person needs onboarding and examples |
| Where it falls short | Does not replace human community management or customer conversations | Can be expensive if the package is mostly basic posting |

## Why this matters

A freelance social media manager may charge $300-$800 per month for a light small-business package and still deliver only 8-12 posts. Broader packages that include Reels, community management, ads, reporting, or strategy can cost much more. That can be worth it when social media is a real sales, reputation, or support channel.

But many owners are not looking for a full marketing department. They need the business to stop looking inactive online. In that case, the expensive part of hiring is often not just the invoice. It is the weekly context handoff: explaining services, sending photos, approving captions, correcting small inaccuracies, and waiting for the person to understand the business.

Glow Social is the better fit when the job is recurring production: turning the business website, services, FAQs, reviews, and local proof into posts the owner can review. The owner still handles the human parts: replying to customers, making judgment calls, and sharing real updates when something important happens.

## When Glow Social is the better choice

Choose Glow Social before hiring a manager when:

- your profiles look quiet because nobody knows what to post
- you want a lower monthly cost before committing to a freelancer or agency
- you already have a website that explains your services, locations, reviews, or offers
- you want posts prepared for approval instead of another blank calendar
- you can spend a few minutes reviewing posts and responding to real comments
- you do not need paid ads, influencer outreach, or complex campaign planning yet

If the decision is really "How do we keep showing up every week without adding another person to manage?" Glow Social is usually the cleaner first move.

## When a social media manager is worth it

Hire a person when the work requires judgment, relationships, or a strategy loop. That includes:

- responding to comments and DMs for the business
- creating original short-form video or on-site photo content
- planning campaigns, launches, promotions, or partnerships
- managing paid ads or influencer relationships
- handling reputation-sensitive topics
- reporting on performance and changing the content plan based on results

A good manager can be worth much more than the monthly fee. The mistake is hiring one for basic posting when the owner mostly needs a reliable production system.

## Real-world example

A mobile pet grooming service compared Glow Social at $99 per month with a freelancer quoting $375 per month. The freelancer offered 8 posts per month, required a 45-minute weekly call to discuss content, and said it would take a few weeks to get familiar with the business.

Glow Social prepared 20 pet-grooming-specific posts from the business website so the owner could review them quickly. The owner still replied to comments herself because those conversations were with real clients. She saved money and kept the relationship-building part in her own voice.

## What most people get wrong

Most comparisons ask whether software can "replace" a social media manager. That is the wrong test. The better question is which job needs to be done right now.

If the job is strategy, campaign thinking, engagement, and judgment, hire a person. If the job is getting consistent posts prepared and published so the business stops looking inactive, start with Glow Social and only hire when the human work becomes valuable enough to justify the cost.
