Roofing is a trust business. Homeowners need to believe you'll show up, do quality work, and stand behind it. Social media is where that trust gets built — or lost.

The problem? Most roofers don't have time to post. Between estimates, crew management, and actual jobs, social media falls to the bottom of the list. And an empty Facebook page tells homeowners you might not be a real company.

## Why Social Media Matters for Roofers

When a homeowner needs a new roof, here's what happens:

1. They ask neighbors or check Google
2. They look at your reviews
3. They check your social media pages
4. If everything looks active and professional, they call

Step 3 is where most roofers lose leads. An inactive page raises doubts, even if your reviews are solid.

## What Roofers Should Be Posting

The best roofing social media content is simple:

- **Before and after photos** of completed jobs
- **Seasonal tips** (spring inspection reminders, storm prep advice)
- **Crew highlights** showing the real people behind the work
- **Customer reviews** shared as posts
- **Educational content** about roofing materials, maintenance, and warning signs

You don't need to be clever. You need to be **consistent**.
