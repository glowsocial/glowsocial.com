Media scheduling is exactly what it sounds like: planning your social media posts in advance and setting them to publish at specific times. It's the first step toward consistent social media — but it's not the whole solution.

## How Social Media Scheduling Works

1. You create content (captions, graphics, links)
2. You load it into a scheduling tool (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later, etc.)
3. You set the date and time for each post
4. The tool publishes automatically at the scheduled time

Simple concept. But here's the catch: scheduling tools handle the **when**. You still handle the **what** — and that's the hard part.

## Why Scheduling Alone Falls Short

Scheduling tools are great for people who already have content ready. For local business owners, the bottleneck isn't scheduling — it's creating the content in the first place.

You still need to:
- Come up with ideas
- Write captions
- Design or find images
- Format for each platform
- Actually sit down and load everything in

That's 5-10 hours per month — and that's why most businesses abandon their scheduling tools within a month.
