After 90 days of consistent posting, a local business usually has a stronger public trust signal: recent posts, more proof, fewer abandoned profiles, and a clearer baseline for what topics drive engagement, profile clicks, and customer conversations.

## Days 1-30: The Business Looks Active Again

The first month is about filling the silence.

Most inconsistent businesses have one obvious problem: the last post is too old. The first 30 days fix that. The profile starts showing current activity, seasonal relevance, service reminders, and proof that the business is operating.

What to measure:

- Posts published.
- Days since last post.
- Profile completeness.
- Google Business Profile activity.
- Early engagement by topic.

## Days 31-60: Patterns Start Appearing

By the second month, you can see which topics are easier to create and which posts get useful signals.

For local businesses, the most useful signals are often not vanity metrics. Saves, profile clicks, calls, website taps, review mentions, and customer comments can matter more than likes.

Common winning topics:

- Reviews.
- Before-and-after proof.
- Customer FAQs.
- Seasonal reminders.
- Local service-area posts.
- Team or behind-the-scenes posts.

## Days 61-90: Trust Compounds

By 90 days, the profile no longer looks abandoned. A potential customer can scroll and see a pattern of expertise, proof, and activity.

That matters because many local customers check social media as a credibility step, not as the original discovery source.

The question is not only "Did this post generate a lead?" It is also "Did this profile help a customer feel safe enough to call?"

## What 90 Days Will Not Prove

Ninety days will not prove that every post directly causes revenue. Local buying journeys are messy. A customer might see a Facebook post, search Google two weeks later, read reviews, and then call.

Use 90 days to prove the system, not the entire business case.

## What To Do Next

After 90 days:

1. Double down on the top 3 topics.
2. Turn reviews and FAQs into recurring posts.
3. Add more Google Business Profile posts.
4. Ask new customers how they heard about you.
5. Compare inbound calls, bookings, and profile actions against the previous 90 days.
