Your social media bio is the first thing potential customers read about your business. It's the two seconds where they decide whether to follow, visit your website, or keep scrolling.

The problem? Every platform has different character limits, different conventions, and different expectations. Writing one great bio is hard enough — writing eight is a full afternoon.

## Why Your Bio Matters More Than You Think

When someone hears about your business through a referral, a Google search, or word of mouth, the first thing they do is look you up on social media. Your bio is what they see.

A good bio tells them:
- What you do (clearly, not cleverly)
- Where you are
- Why they should choose you
- How to take the next step

A bad bio? It's either empty, generic ("Welcome to our page!"), or hasn't been updated since 2019.

## The Character Limit Problem

Each platform gives you a different amount of space:

| Platform | Character Limit | What Works |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | 150 | Punchy, location + key service + CTA |
| Twitter / X | 160 | Concise, expertise-led |
| Bluesky | 256 | Room for personality |
| Facebook | 255 | Service-focused, local keywords |
| LinkedIn | 2,000 | Professional, detailed, SEO-rich |
| Google Business | 750 | SEO-optimized, all services listed |
| Pinterest | 500 | Discovery-focused, keyword-rich |
| Threads | 150 | Conversational, personality-forward |

Writing all eight manually means rewriting and trimming and adjusting character by character. That's tedious, and most business owners give up after two platforms.

## Tips for Great Social Media Bios

Whether you use our tool or write them yourself, here's what works:

### Lead with what you do, not who you are
- ❌ "Welcome to Johnson & Sons! Established 1987."
- ✅ "Emergency plumbing in Phoenix — same-day service, no trip charges."

### Include your location
Local businesses live and die by "[service] near me" searches. Put your city in your bio.

### End with a call to action
Tell people what to do next: "Book online," "Call for a free estimate," "DM us for hours."

### Match the platform's vibe
LinkedIn bios should sound professional. Instagram bios should be punchy. Twitter bios can be clever. Don't use the same bio everywhere — the [Profile Kit](/setup) handles this for you.

### Update it seasonally
Your bio from two years ago probably mentions services you've changed, hours you've updated, or a phone number that's different. Set a quarterly reminder to review.
