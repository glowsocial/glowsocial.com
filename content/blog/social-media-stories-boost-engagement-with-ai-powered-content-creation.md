Stories disappear after 24 hours. But their impact on your business doesn't.

For local businesses, Stories on Instagram and Facebook are one of the most effective ways to stay visible. They appear at the top of the app, they feel personal, and they reward consistent posting.

## Why Stories Work for Local Businesses

Stories feel more casual than feed posts. That's exactly what makes them effective for local businesses:

- Customers get a behind-the-scenes look at your business
- You can show work in progress, daily operations, and your team
- Stories create urgency — they disappear, so people watch now
- The algorithm favors accounts that use Stories regularly

## Types of Stories That Drive Engagement

**Behind-the-scenes content:** Show your team at work. A roofer up on a job, a baker pulling bread from the oven, a mechanic under the hood.

**Quick tips:** Share one piece of advice related to your industry. "How to spot water damage early" or "The one thing to check before winter" — simple, helpful, and shareable.

**Customer spotlights:** Share a review or a quick thank-you to a recent customer.

**Polls and questions:** Ask your audience something simple. "Coffee or tea?" or "What service should we feature next?" Both drive interaction.

## The Time Problem with Stories

Stories need to feel fresh and frequent. That means creating content daily — and for most business owners, that's simply not realistic.

This is where AI-powered tools can help. Instead of manually creating Stories every day, smart automation handles the heavy lifting while keeping content personalized to your brand.
