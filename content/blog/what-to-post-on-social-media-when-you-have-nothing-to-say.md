# What to Post on Social Media When You Have Nothing to Say

You sit down to write a post for your business and your mind goes completely blank. You've been doing this for weeks. What else is there to say about what you do?

This happens to every business owner who handles their own social media. It's not a creativity problem. It's a system problem. Here's how to solve it permanently.

## The Root Cause: You're Starting From Zero Every Time

Most business owners approach each post as a fresh creation — staring at a blank caption box and trying to think of something. This is the hardest possible way to do it.

The fix is to never start from zero. Build a content bank, work from categories, and batch your ideas one time per month rather than daily.

## 30+ Content Ideas That Never Run Out

### From Your Own Expertise (Never Runs Dry)

Every question a customer has ever asked you is a post:
- "We get this question all the time: [common question]. Here's the answer."
- "[Myth about your industry] — why this is wrong and what's actually true"
- "The one mistake most people make with [service you offer]"
- "How we decide whether to [do X or Y] and why it matters"
- "What does [industry term] actually mean in plain English?"

You have a decade of expertise. Your customers have exactly zero of it. Everything you know that they don't is a post.

### Behind the Scenes (Unlimited Material)

Whatever is happening in your business right now is content:
- A job in progress (before shot, process shot, after shot — three posts from one job)
- The tools or equipment you use and why you chose them
- A typical day starting at 7am
- The checklist your team runs through before a job
- A team member's backstory and why they love this work
- The thing that surprised you about this business when you first started

### Customer Stories (Ask More Often Than You Think)

One happy customer = weeks of content:
- Written testimonial with permission to share
- Before/after if your work is visual
- "We helped [first name] with [specific problem] — here's what changed"
- A follow-up months later ("Remember [customer]? Here's how things look now")
- A customer referral story ("Her neighbor saw our work and called us the next day")

### Educational Content (Drives Trust and Shares)

Teach something genuinely useful:
- Seasonal maintenance tip related to your service
- "Signs you need to call a [professional in your field] (and signs you don't)"
- "How to choose a good [your profession]" — yes, teach them how to evaluate you
- A common DIY mistake that ends up costing more
- When to do something yourself vs. call a professional

### Local Community Content (Builds Loyalty)

Tie into your local community:
- A neighboring business you love (tag them for a mutual boost)
- A local event you're sponsoring or attending
- A seasonal happening in your area (first day of summer, local school sports playoffs)
- A local charity you support
- "Proud to serve [your city] for [X] years"

### Promotional Content (Use Sparingly, Do it Right)

One in five posts can be directly promotional:
- Current availability ("We have 3 openings next week — book now")
- Seasonal service reminder ("Time for your annual [X]")
- A specific offer with a deadline
- Your most popular service with a clear next step
- A FAQ about pricing, with honest answers

## The Batch-Creation System

Once a month, sit down with this list and your calendar. Block 90 minutes. Fill out one idea for each category above. You'll end up with 30+ ideas in a single session.

Then — crucially — write or photograph as much as possible in that same session. Writing 10 captions in a flow state takes less time than writing 1 caption 10 times across 10 different mornings.

If you use a scheduling tool, load everything into a queue and you're done for the month.
