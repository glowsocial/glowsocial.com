## What Home Service Businesses Need from Social Media

Home service social media has specific requirements:


<ul>
<li><strong>Trust building:</strong> Homeowners want to see your work before letting you in their house</li>
<li><strong>Before/after results:</strong> Project photos that demonstrate quality</li>
<li><strong>Local presence:</strong> Showing up when neighbors talk about contractors</li>
<li><strong>Seasonal reminders:</strong> HVAC tune-ups, fall cleanups, spring projects</li>
<li><strong>Time efficiency:</strong> Owners are on job sites, not at desks</li>
</ul>

## Content That Works for Home Services

<ul>
<li><strong>Before/after project photos:</strong> Your most powerful content—shows quality and transformation</li>
<li><strong>Work in progress:</strong> Behind-the-scenes of jobs shows expertise and care</li>
<li><strong>Seasonal tips:</strong> "Time for HVAC tune-up," "Prepare your yard for winter," etc.</li>
<li><strong>Team features:</strong> Introduce crew members, show professionalism</li>
<li><strong>Customer testimonials:</strong> Reviews and thank-you notes (with permission)</li>
<li><strong>Educational content:</strong> How to spot problems, when to call a pro, maintenance tips</li>
<li><strong>Local community:</strong> Involvement in local events, neighborhood support</li>
</ul>

## The Before/After Photo Strategy

For home service businesses, before/after photos are essential. Here's how to capture them:


<strong>Quick workflow for job sites:</strong>

<ol>
<li>Take "before" photo when you arrive (takes 30 seconds)</li>
<li>Take "after" photo when you finish</li>
<li>Send to whoever handles your social media (or yourself)</li>
<li>Create simple before/after posts weekly or monthly</li>
</ol>

## DIY vs Automated: Home Service Reality Check

<strong>DIY works if:</strong>

<ul>
<li>You have office staff who can handle content creation</li>
<li>Someone has 3-5 hours monthly for social media</li>
<li>You're disciplined about capturing photos on every job</li>
</ul>

<strong>Automated posting works if:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Everyone is on job sites, not at desks</li>
<li>Marketing always falls to "someday when we have time"</li>
<li>You've tried DIY but it never sticks</li>
<li>Slow seasons hurt because you didn't stay visible during busy times</li>
</ul>
