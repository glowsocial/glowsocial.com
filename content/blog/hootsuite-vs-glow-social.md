## What Hootsuite Does Well

Hootsuite is powerful social media management software:

<ul>
<li>Manage dozens of social accounts from one dashboard</li>
<li>Advanced analytics and custom reports</li>
<li>Social listening and monitoring</li>
<li>Team collaboration with approval workflows</li>
<li>Integration with 150+ apps</li>
<li>Best-time-to-post optimization</li>
</ul>

<strong>Best for:</strong> Marketing teams managing multiple brands or large social presence.

<strong>The catch:</strong> All of this power means complexity. Most small businesses use 10% of what Hootsuite offers — and still have to create all their own content.

## What Glow Social Does Well

Glow Social takes the opposite approach:

<ul>
<li>5-minute setup — answer questions about your business</li>
<li>AI creates 12 custom posts per month</li>
<li>Automatic publishing to Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and TikTok</li>
<li>No dashboard to learn</li>
<li>No content calendar to manage</li>
<li>No team needed</li>
</ul>

<strong>Best for:</strong> Business owners who want posting handled, not managed.

<strong>The catch:</strong> No advanced analytics, no team features, no social listening. It does one thing — keep your social media active — and does it well.

## When to Choose Hootsuite

<ul>
<li>You have a marketing team or dedicated social media person</li>
<li>You manage multiple brands or clients</li>
<li>You need advanced analytics and reporting</li>
<li>Social media is a core revenue driver requiring strategy</li>
<li>You have $99+/month budget for social tools</li>
</ul>

## When to Choose Glow Social

<ul>
<li>You're a one-person or small team business</li>
<li>You don't have time to create content</li>
<li>You want consistent posting without managing a tool</li>
<li>Your budget is under $100/month</li>
<li>You've tried Hootsuite (or similar) and stopped using it</li>
</ul>
