## What Eats the Most Time?

For most small business owners, time disappears into these areas:


<strong>1. Deciding what to post (2-4 hours/month)</strong>

The blank page problem: staring at your phone wondering "what should I post today?" This mental overhead often costs more time than actual content creation.


<strong>2. Writing captions (2-3 hours/month)</strong>

Crafting posts that don't sound generic or salesy. Most people overthink this.


<strong>3. Creating or finding graphics (2-4 hours/month)</strong>

Designing images in Canva, taking photos, organizing visual assets.


<strong>4. Scheduling and posting (1-2 hours/month)</strong>

Loading content into a scheduler, figuring out timing, managing the calendar.


<strong>5. Engagement (1-3 hours/month)</strong>

Responding to comments and messages. (This is the one part that actually matters for community building.)


## How Much Time is Actually Necessary?

Here's what the research says about posting frequency:


<ul>
<li><strong>Minimum effective:</strong> 3-4 posts per week keeps you visible</li>
<li><strong>Sweet spot for most small businesses:</strong> 1 post per day</li>
<li><strong>More than 2 per day:</strong> Usually diminishing returns</li>
</ul>

That means 12-30 posts per month covers most small business needs.
