People search for "Later free plan" because they want a social media scheduler without adding another monthly bill.

That is reasonable. The confusing part is that Later's public pricing page leads with a 14-day free trial for paid plans, while Later's help center also describes a limited Free plan for creators. Those are not the same thing.

Here is the clean small-business version.

## Direct Answer: Later Free Plan, Trial, and Pricing

As of this June 22, 2026 refresh, Later has a **limited Free plan** and a **14-day free trial** for paid social media management plans. Later's help center says the Free plan lets creators schedule a limited number of posts each month or participate in brand campaigns. Later's public pricing page highlights paid plans: Starter, Growth, and Scale.

If you are looking for the strongest ongoing free scheduler for a small business, compare:

- **Meta Business Suite** for free Facebook and Instagram scheduling.
- **Buffer** for a free plan with up to 3 channels and 10 scheduled posts per channel.
- **Publer** for a free account-based scheduler with limits.
- **Metricool** for a free 1-brand analytics-and-scheduling plan with limits.

Later is still useful. It is strongest when you already create visual content and want a better way to plan Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, and similar channels.

If you do not already have posts ready, the free-plan question is not the real issue. The real issue is content supply. A free scheduler cannot fill an empty calendar.

## What Is a Later Social Set?

A Later Social Set includes one profile from each supported platform:

- Instagram
- Facebook
- Threads
- Pinterest
- TikTok
- LinkedIn
- YouTube
- Snapchat

Starter includes 1 Social Set. Growth includes 2. Scale includes 6.

That model works well when you use several of those visual and creator-oriented platforms. It works less cleanly if you only care about two or three profiles, or if Google Business Profile posting is important to your local visibility.

## When Later Is Worth It

Later is worth it when:

- Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, or visual planning are central.
- You already create photos, Reels, TikToks, or product images.
- You want a visual calendar and grid-style workflow.
- You need Link in Bio or creator-style campaign planning.
- You have enough content to keep a calendar full.

Later can be a good fit for boutiques, salons, med spas, restaurants with strong food photography, photographers, creators, and visual brands.

## When Later Is Not Worth It

Later is not worth it when:

- You do not already have content to schedule.
- Your social accounts go quiet because you are busy.
- Google Business Profile posting is a priority.
- You mostly need simple Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn posting.
- You want posts written and designed for you.

In those cases, compare [free social media scheduler options](/blog/free-social-media-scheduler), [the cheapest social media scheduler options](/blog/cheapest-social-media-scheduler), and [whether a scheduler is enough](/resources/questions/is-a-social-media-scheduler-enough-for-a-small-business).

## The Hidden Cost of Later

Later Starter may cost $18.75/month on annual billing, but the true cost depends on how much content work stays with you.

A realistic monthly workflow can include:

- Taking or sourcing photos.
- Recording short videos.
- Writing captions.
- Choosing hashtags.
- Building the visual calendar.
- Editing for each platform.
- Reviewing analytics.

If that takes 5 hours per month and your time is worth $25/hour, the true cost looks more like:

**$18.75 + $125 in owner time = $143.75/month**

That can still be a good deal if you enjoy content creation. It is a bad deal if you keep avoiding the calendar.
