Later is a visual-first social media scheduler. It is strongest when you already create photos and videos and need a better way to plan, schedule, and analyze them.

The pricing can be confusing because Later uses **Social Sets**, not simple per-channel pricing. It also has a limited Free plan, while the public pricing page focuses heavily on a 14-day trial for paid plans.

Here is the clean version.

## Direct Answer: Later Pricing and Free Plan

As of May 2026, Later's paid social scheduling plans start at:

- **Starter:** $18.75/month billed yearly
- **Growth:** $37.50/month billed yearly
- **Scale:** $82.50/month billed yearly

Later also has a limited **Free plan** for basic scheduling and Link in Bio use, plus a **14-day free trial** for paid plans.

For most small businesses, the important question is not "Can I afford Later?" It is "Do I already create enough visual content to make Later useful?"

If yes, Later can be a strong visual planning tool. If no, a scheduler may just give you a prettier blank calendar.

## What Is a Later Social Set?

A Social Set includes one profile from each platform Later supports:

- Instagram
- Facebook
- Threads
- Pinterest
- TikTok
- LinkedIn
- YouTube
- Snapchat

So Later Starter gives you 1 profile from each supported platform. Growth gives you 2 of each. Scale gives you 6 of each.

This is different from Buffer, where you pay per channel. If you want to compare the two models, read [Later vs Buffer](/blog/later-vs-buffer-differences-comparison) or [Buffer pricing and free plan limits](/blog/buffer-pricing-free-plan-limits-2026).

## Does Later Have a Free Plan?

Yes, Later's help center describes a limited Free plan. It is designed for basic creator use, introductory scheduling, Link in Bio, and lightweight access to Later.

But Later's public pricing page focuses on the 14-day free trial for paid plans. So for business planning, think of Later like this:

- **Free plan:** useful for testing and very light scheduling
- **14-day trial:** useful for testing paid features
- **Starter and above:** where most serious business use begins

If you need a generous ongoing free scheduler, Buffer is usually the clearer option because its free plan is explicit: up to 3 channels and 10 scheduled posts per channel.

## Later Plan Details

### Starter

Starter is for solo users and casual scheduling.

It includes:

- 1 Social Set
- 1 user
- 30 posts per profile per month
- 5 AI credits per month
- Platform analytics up to 3 months
- Link in Bio
- Basic planning and publishing tools

Choose Starter if you are a solo creator or local business that already has photos and videos ready.

### Growth

Growth is for small teams that need collaboration and more publishing room.

It includes:

- 2 Social Sets
- 2 users
- 180 posts per profile per month
- 50 AI credits per month
- Platform analytics up to 1 year
- Collaboration and approval workflows
- Social inbox
- UGC collection

Choose Growth if multiple people help manage your social media or if you need approvals.

### Scale

Scale is for bigger content operations.

It includes:

- 6 Social Sets
- 4 users
- Unlimited posts
- 100 AI credits per month
- Platform analytics up to 2 years
- Custom analytics
- Competitive benchmarking
- Priority support

Choose Scale if you manage multiple brands, locations, or content-heavy calendars.

## When Later Is Worth It

Later is worth it if:

- Instagram, TikTok, or Pinterest are central to your marketing
- You create visual content regularly
- You want to preview your Instagram grid
- You need Link in Bio
- You manage UGC, Reels, or visual campaigns
- You have enough content to fill a calendar

Later is especially useful for photographers, boutiques, restaurants with strong food photography, salons, med spas, creators, and visual brands.

## When Later Is Not Worth It

Later is not worth it if:

- You do not already create visual content
- Your social pages are empty because you lack time, not tools
- You need Google Business Profile posting
- You want posts written and designed for you
- You are a local service business that just needs consistent presence

For many small businesses, the hard part is not scheduling. It is creating something worth scheduling.

## The Hidden Cost of Later

Later's Starter plan may cost under $20/month on annual billing, but your time still matters.

A realistic monthly workflow includes:

- Taking or sourcing photos
- Recording short videos
- Writing captions
- Choosing hashtags
- Building the visual calendar
- Editing for each platform
- Reviewing analytics

If that takes 5 hours per month and your time is worth $25/hour, the true cost of Later Starter is closer to:

**$18.75 + $125 in owner time = $143.75/month**

That may still be worth it if you enjoy content creation. It is not worth it if you keep avoiding the calendar.
