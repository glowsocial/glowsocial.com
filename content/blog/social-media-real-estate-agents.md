Real estate agents need consistent social media to stay top-of-mind with potential buyers and sellers, but most agents are too busy showing homes and closing deals to create content. This guide covers what works for real estate specifically, not generic marketing advice.


## What Real Estate Agents Need from Social Media

Real estate social media has specific requirements:


<ul>
<li><strong>Consistency:</strong> Staying visible between transactions when you're not actively closing</li>
<li><strong>Local knowledge:</strong> Neighborhood expertise, market updates, community content</li>
<li><strong>Professional presence:</strong> Building trust before the first meeting</li>
<li><strong>Time efficiency:</strong> Agents work irregular hours and don't have dedicated marketing time</li>
<li><strong>Listings (sometimes):</strong> Though this often happens through MLS feeds and dedicated real estate platforms</li>
</ul>

## Content That Works for Real Estate

<ul>
<li><strong>Market updates:</strong> Local stats, trends, what buyers/sellers should know</li>
<li><strong>Neighborhood spotlights:</strong> Schools, restaurants, parks, community events</li>
<li><strong>Buying/selling tips:</strong> Educational content that positions you as an expert</li>
<li><strong>Behind the scenes:</strong> Open house prep, closing day celebrations, home staging</li>
<li><strong>Personal brand:</strong> Why you do real estate, community involvement, client success stories</li>
<li><strong>Listings:</strong> New on market, price changes, just sold (but don't make every post a listing)</li>
</ul>

## DIY vs Automated: Agent Reality Check

Be honest about your schedule:


<strong>DIY scheduling works if:</strong>

<ul>
<li>You have designated marketing time (3-5 hours/month)</li>
<li>You have a transaction coordinator or assistant who handles content</li>
<li>You enjoy creating content and won't skip it when busy</li>
</ul>

<strong>Automated posting works if:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Your schedule varies wildly based on client needs</li>
<li>You've tried DIY and always fall off when transactions pick up</li>
<li>You'd rather spend your time prospecting and closing</li>
</ul>
