## What Restaurants Need from Social Media

Restaurant social media has specific requirements:


<ul>
<li><strong>Visual content:</strong> Food photos and behind-the-scenes glimpses</li>
<li><strong>Timely posts:</strong> Daily specials, weekend brunch, holiday hours</li>
<li><strong>Local reach:</strong> Targeting people within driving distance</li>
<li><strong>Consistency:</strong> Staying visible during slow seasons, not just when you remember to post</li>
<li><strong>Time efficiency:</strong> Owners and managers are already working 60+ hour weeks</li>
</ul>

## DIY vs Automated: Restaurant Reality Check

Be honest about your situation:


<strong>You have time for DIY if:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Someone on staff can dedicate 3-5 hours/month to content</li>
<li>You have good photos of your food on a regular basis</li>
<li>You won't skip posting when the restaurant gets busy</li>
</ul>

<strong>You need automated posting if:</strong>

<ul>
<li>You're already working 60+ hours/week</li>
<li>Social media always falls to the bottom of the list</li>
<li>You've started posting "plans" that never lasted</li>
<li>You don't have designated marketing staff</li>
</ul>

## Content Ideas for Restaurant Social Media

Whether you're creating content yourself or reviewing automated posts, here's what works:


<ul>
<li><strong>Food photos:</strong> Signature dishes, daily specials, seasonal items</li>
<li><strong>Behind the scenes:</strong> Kitchen prep, team members, suppliers</li>
<li><strong>Community:</strong> Local events, neighborhood shoutouts, regular customers (with permission)</li>
<li><strong>Practical info:</strong> Hours, reservations, parking, special accommodations</li>
<li><strong>Timely content:</strong> Holiday hours, seasonal menus, weather-related posts</li>
</ul>
