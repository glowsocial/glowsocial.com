For local businesses—restaurants, salons, contractors, retail—<strong>Google Business Profile often drives more leads than social media</strong>. When someone searches "plumber near me" or "best coffee shop downtown," they're finding you on Google, not scrolling Instagram. So where should you actually focus?


Here's how to prioritize when you can't do everything.


## What Each Channel Actually Does

<strong>Google Business Profile:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Shows up when people search for your business type in your area</li>
<li>Displays reviews, hours, photos, and contact info</li>
<li>Drives phone calls, directions, and website visits</li>
<li>Reaches people actively looking for what you offer</li>
</ul>

<strong>Social Media (Facebook, Instagram, etc.):</strong>

<ul>
<li>Builds familiarity with people who already follow you</li>
<li>Keeps you top-of-mind between purchases</li>
<li>Showcases personality and culture</li>
<li>Reaches people scrolling casually (not actively searching)</li>
</ul>

## Which Drives More Local Business?

For most local businesses, Google Business Profile has higher immediate impact:


<strong>Google Business Profile advantage:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Reaches high-intent searchers ("I need a plumber NOW")</li>
<li>Reviews visible at the decision moment</li>
<li>Free, no content creation required</li>
<li>Directly generates calls and visits</li>
</ul>

<strong>Social media advantage:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Builds long-term brand recognition</li>
<li>Creates community connection</li>
<li>Good for repeat purchases and referrals</li>
<li>Showcases work that reviews can't show</li>
</ul>

If you had to pick one: Google Business Profile usually wins for local lead generation.


## The Priority Stack

For local businesses with limited time, focus in this order:


<strong>Priority 1: Google Business Profile (essential)</strong>

<ul>
<li>Claim and verify your listing</li>
<li>Complete every field (hours, services, photos)</li>
<li>Respond to every review</li>
<li>Post updates occasionally</li>
<li>Ask happy customers for reviews</li>
</ul>

<strong>Priority 2: Review collection (high impact)</strong>

<ul>
<li>Ask every satisfied customer to leave a Google review</li>
<li>Make it easy (send a direct link)</li>
<li>Respond to all reviews (positive and negative)</li>
<li>This improves Google ranking AND social proof</li>
</ul>

<strong>Priority 3: Basic social presence (maintain visibility)</strong>

<ul>
<li>Facebook page + Instagram at minimum</li>
<li>Consistent posting (or automated with Glow Social for $49/month)</li>
<li>Share your best work</li>
</ul>

## What to Do on Google Business Profile

<strong>Weekly (10 minutes):</strong>

<ul>
<li>Check for new reviews → respond to each one</li>
<li>Check for messages or questions → respond quickly</li>
</ul>

<strong>Monthly (30 minutes):</strong>

<ul>
<li>Add new photos of recent work</li>
<li>Post an update (special, event, news)</li>
<li>Update any changed information</li>
</ul>

<strong>Ongoing:</strong>

<ul>
<li>Ask every happy customer for a review</li>
<li>Send them the direct link by text or email</li>
</ul>
