You have 150 characters to convince someone to follow your business on Instagram. That's less than a tweet. Less than this paragraph.

Most businesses waste these 150 characters on generic phrases like "Welcome to our page!" or "Quality service since 2005." That tells a potential customer absolutely nothing useful.

Here's how to write an Instagram bio that actually works.

## The Formula: What Every Business Instagram Bio Needs

Every effective business bio has four components, in this order:

### 1. What you do (be specific)
Not "helping people achieve their goals." Not "your trusted partner."

- ✅ "Emergency plumbing — Phoenix metro"
- ✅ "Hair color specialist · balayage + vivids"
- ✅ "Thai street food · lunch & dinner"
- ❌ "Welcome to our page!"
- ❌ "Quality you can trust ✨"

### 2. Where you are
Local businesses need their city or neighborhood in the bio. This is how people decide if you're near them.

Use 📍 to save characters: `📍 Downtown Phoenix`

### 3. What makes you different (one thing)
Pick one differentiator. Not three. One.

- "Same-day appointments"
- "Family-owned since 1998"
- "Vegan menu available"
- "Free estimates"

### 4. Call to action
Tell people what to do. Instagram gives you one clickable link — make the CTA match it.

- "Book online ↓"
- "Free quote: link below"
- "Order online 👇"

## Real Examples

Emergency plumbing · Phoenix metro

## Common Mistakes

**Being clever instead of clear.** "Where flavor meets passion meets innovation" tells me nothing. "Wood-fired pizza · downtown Phoenix" tells me everything.

**Using corporate language.** "Solutions provider" and "innovative approach" are meaningless on Instagram. Talk like a human.

**Ignoring the link in bio.** Your bio CTA should match your link. If your CTA says "Book online" but your link goes to your homepage, you'll lose people.

**Copy-pasting the same bio everywhere.** Instagram's 150-character limit is not the same as LinkedIn's 2,000-character About section. Each platform needs its own version.
