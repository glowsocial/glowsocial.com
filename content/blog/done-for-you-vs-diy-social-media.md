The choice between DIY social media tools and done-for-you solutions comes down to one question: Do you want to do your own social media or have it handled? DIY tools like Buffer and Hootsuite cost less but require 5-10 hours monthly. Automated software like <strong>Glow Social</strong> costs $49/month but requires almost no ongoing time.


Here's how to decide which approach fits your situation.


## What "DIY" Actually Means

DIY social media tools help you post more efficiently, but you're still doing the work:


<ul>
<li><strong>Content creation:</strong> You write captions, create graphics, decide what to post</li>
<li><strong>Scheduling:</strong> You load content into a calendar and set posting times</li>
<li><strong>Management:</strong> You monitor what's working and adjust your strategy</li>
<li><strong>Consistency:</strong> You're responsible for keeping the content pipeline full</li>
</ul>

Popular DIY tools: Buffer, Hootsuite, Later, Sprout Social, Meta Business Suite


## What "Done-For-You" Actually Means

Done-for-you options handle social media without requiring your ongoing time:


<ul>
<li><strong>Content creation:</strong> Software or service creates posts for you</li>
<li><strong>Scheduling:</strong> Automatic—happens without your involvement</li>
<li><strong>Management:</strong> Minimal—occasional reviews if you want them</li>
<li><strong>Consistency:</strong> Posts go out whether you're busy or not</li>
</ul>

Options: Glow Social (software, $49/month), freelance social media managers ($300-500/month), agencies ($1,000-3,000/month)

## When DIY Makes Sense

<strong>Choose DIY social media if:</strong>


<ul>
<li><strong>You have time:</strong> 5-10 hours per month isn't a problem</li>
<li><strong>You enjoy it:</strong> Content creation energizes rather than drains you</li>
<li><strong>You want full control:</strong> Every post reflects exactly what you want to say</li>
<li><strong>You're building skill:</strong> Learning social media marketing is a business goal</li>
<li><strong>Budget is extremely tight:</strong> Free tools work if you have time</li>
</ul>

DIY works well for people who see social media as part of their job, not a distraction from it.


## When Done-For-You Makes Sense

<strong>Choose done-for-you if:</strong>


<ul>
<li><strong>You don't have time:</strong> 5-10 hours monthly isn't realistic</li>
<li><strong>You don't enjoy it:</strong> Content creation feels like a chore</li>
<li><strong>Consistency is the problem:</strong> You've tried DIY and always fall off</li>
<li><strong>Your time is valuable:</strong> Those hours could be spent on revenue-generating work</li>
<li><strong>You want visibility without the work:</strong> Results matter more than control</li>
</ul>

Done-for-you works well for business owners who see social media as necessary but not their strength.


## The Hidden Cost of DIY

DIY tools appear cheaper, but calculate your time cost:


<strong>Example:</strong> 8 hours/month × $50/hour (your effective rate) = $400 in time


Add tool costs ($15-50/month) and DIY actually costs $415-450/month when you count your time.


Compare to Glow Social at $49/month with minimal time, and the math often favors automation—especially for business owners whose time could generate more revenue elsewhere.


## What About Quality?

Common concern: "Won't my content be better if I create it myself?"


Maybe. But consider:


<ul>
<li><strong>Inconsistent good content loses to consistent okay content.</strong> Posting regularly matters more than occasional brilliance.</li>
<li><strong>Most DIY content isn't actually better.</strong> Business owners creating content under time pressure often produce rushed work anyway.</li>
<li><strong>Visibility beats perfection.</strong> Customers can't hire you if they don't see you.</li>
</ul>

If you genuinely create excellent content and enjoy it, DIY makes sense. If you're creating mediocre content while resenting the time it takes, automation might produce similar results with less frustration.


## The Hybrid Approach

Many businesses combine approaches:


<ul>
<li>Glow Social handles baseline posting (12 posts/month, automatic)</li>
<li>You add manual posts when you have something specific to share</li>
<li>Consistent visibility without the pressure to constantly create</li>
</ul>

This gives you presence without obligation.


## Making the Decision

<strong>Ask yourself:</strong>


<ol>
<li>Do I have 5-10 hours per month for social media? (If no → done-for-you)</li>
<li>Do I enjoy creating content? (If no → done-for-you)</li>
<li>Have I tried DIY and stuck with it? (If no → done-for-you)</li>
<li>Is my time worth more than $10/hour? (If yes → done-for-you often makes financial sense)</li>
</ol>

If you answered "no" to most of these, automated posting is probably your better option.
