When it comes to local business social media, everyone's fighting in the wrong arena.

According to our newly published [State of Local Business Social Media 2026](/research) report—which analyzed over 14,000 real social posts from local service businesses—there's a massive disconnect between where businesses spend their time and where they actually acquire customers.

We call it the **Google Business Profile Posting Gap**.

## The Data Defining the Gap

We looked at the average weekly posting frequency across major platforms. Here's what we found:

*   **Facebook:** 2.58 posts per week
*   **LinkedIn:** 2.49 posts per week
*   **Instagram:** 2.28 posts per week
*   **Google Business Profile:** 0.12 posts per week

Local businesses are treating Google Business Profile (GBP) like a static phonebook listing. They set it up, get a few reviews, and then abandon it to go post reels on Instagram.

But when we looked at the *engagement rates* (which for GBP includes high-intent actions like website clicks, calls, and direction requests), the narrative flipped entirely:

*   **Pinterest:** 7.7%
*   **Google Business Profile:** 4.14%
*   **Threads:** 2.61%
*   **LinkedIn:** 2.24%
*   **Instagram:** 2.14%

**The takeaway is staggering:** Local businesses are posting to Facebook *21 times more frequently* than Google Business Profile, despite GBP having more than double the engagement and conversion intent.

## Why This Gap Exists

Why are business owners ignoring such a powerful channel?

1.  **It doesn't feel like "Social Media":** GBP lacks the dopamine-hit of an Instagram feed. There are no followers to count or viral trends to chase.
2.  **Tool fragmentation:** Most legacy social media scheduling tools treat GBP as an afterthought, making it difficult to post updates consistently.
3.  **The "Set and Forget" myth:** Too many marketers advise local businesses to simply optimize their GBP profile once and rely solely on reviews.

## The Asymmetric Advantage

This gap represents the single largest opportunity in local SEO today.

Because your competitors aren't posting to Google Business Profile, the platform is starved for frequent, localized content. When a homeowner searches for "plumber near me," Google doesn't just look at reviews and proximity; it looks at recency and relevance. 

Active GBP Update posts signal to Google that a business is currently operating, engaged, and relevant. Furthermore, these posts appear directly in the Knowledge Panel on the Search Engine Results Page (SERP)—meaning you capture attention *before* the user even clicks through to your website.

If you are a local service provider (plumber, roofer, real estate agent, etc.), a weekly Google Business Profile post gives you an asymmetric advantage. You are playing in an uncontested space.
