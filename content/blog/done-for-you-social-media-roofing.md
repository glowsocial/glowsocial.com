Roofing companies face a unique marketing challenge: your best advertising is the work you do, but most homeowners will never see it unless you share it online.

Done-for-you social media solves this. Instead of hiring a $2,000/month agency or trying to figure out Instagram between jobs, you get professional content published automatically. For most roofers, it's the only approach that actually stays consistent month after month.

## Why Roofers Need Social Media in 2026

The roofing industry has shifted dramatically in how homeowners find and choose contractors. Here's what the numbers look like:

**88% of consumers** check online reviews and social media before hiring a home service provider. When a homeowner's neighbor recommends two roofers, the one with an active Facebook page and recent project photos wins the call almost every time.

**Google Business Profile** has become the primary discovery channel for local service businesses. Regular posts on your GBP listing improve your visibility in "roofer near me" searches — which is where most residential roofing leads start.

**Insurance claims and storm season** create surges of demand where speed matters. The roofer who already has a trusted, visible online presence closes these leads faster than one who's invisible online.

An active social media presence tells potential customers: "We're busy, we're professional, and we're doing quality work right now." An empty or outdated profile tells them: "We might not be around anymore."

## Content That Works for Roofing Companies

Not every type of content performs equally for roofers. Here's what consistently gets engagement and generates leads:

### Before-and-After Project Photos
This is the single highest-performing content type for any roofing company. A side-by-side of a worn, damaged roof next to a clean new installation tells the story instantly. No long caption needed — the transformation speaks for itself.

### Storm Damage Education
Posts that explain how to identify hail damage, what to do after a storm, or when to file an insurance claim position you as the local expert. These posts get saved and shared, which means they keep working for you long after they're published.

### Seasonal Maintenance Tips
"5 things to check on your roof before winter" or "Why spring is the best time for a roof inspection" — these posts are useful, shareable, and create natural demand for your services at exactly the right time of year.

### Customer Testimonials
Share your best reviews as social media posts. Add a photo of the completed project, and you've combined social proof with visual evidence of your quality work. This combination is more persuasive than any advertisement.

### Team and Process Content
Showing your crew at work, highlighting safety practices, or explaining how you approach a complex repair humanizes your business. Homeowners want to know who's going to be on their roof — and seeing a professional, friendly team builds confidence.

### Material and Product Education
Posts explaining the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles, the benefits of impact-resistant roofing, or why proper ventilation matters demonstrate expertise. Homeowners who learn from your content call you first when they need the work done.
