Short answer: you don't need a degree to manage social media for your business. You need consistency, relevant content, and the time to actually do it.

## What a Social Media Management Degree Teaches

Social media marketing programs cover strategy, analytics, content creation, advertising, and platform-specific tactics. For aspiring marketing professionals, this education is valuable.

But for local business owners? You don't need to understand engagement algorithms or audience segmentation theory. You need posts to go up regularly so customers know you're still in business.

## What Actually Matters for Local Business Social Media

1. **Consistent posting** — 3+ times per week across your key platforms
2. **Relevant content** — posts that reflect your actual services and location
3. **Professional presentation** — decent graphics and clear captions
4. **Platform coverage** — Facebook, Instagram, Google Business Profile at minimum
5. **Authenticity** — content that sounds like your business, not a marketing textbook

## The Skills vs. Time Trade-Off

You probably already know what to post. The problem isn't knowledge — it's time. Between running your business, managing employees, and serving customers, social media gets pushed to the bottom of the list.
