## Direct Answer

Empty or abandoned social profiles make customers hesitate. Consistent posting shows that the business is active, responsive, and still serving customers. For local businesses, that trust signal can matter before a customer calls, books, or requests a quote.

## What an Empty Profile Communicates

An inactive profile can accidentally say:

- The business might be closed.
- The team might be too busy or disorganized.
- The information might be outdated.
- No one is paying attention.
- Competitors may be a safer choice.

That may be unfair, but customers make quick judgments when comparing local businesses.

## What Consistent Posting Communicates

Recent posts show:

- The business is active.
- The team has current customers.
- The company knows its work.
- The owner cares about public reputation.
- The service is still available.

The content does not need to be perfect. It needs to be current, useful, and credible.

## Customer Scenario

A homeowner searches for a contractor. Two businesses have similar reviews.

Business A has a Facebook page where the last post is from eight months ago.

Business B has recent posts showing job photos, maintenance tips, a customer review, and a reminder about seasonal service.

Even if both companies are equally skilled, Business B feels less risky.

## The Minimum Trust Baseline

Most local businesses should maintain:

- Recent posts within the last 7-10 days.
- Accurate hours and contact details.
- Current service descriptions.
- Reviews or testimonials.
- Clear links back to the website.
- Google Business Profile activity.

## What To Post When You Have Nothing New

Use repeatable content:

- A customer FAQ.
- A review.
- A seasonal reminder.
- A service explanation.
- A team photo.
- A before-and-after example.
- A Google Business Profile update.

You do not need breaking news every week. You need useful proof that the business is alive.
