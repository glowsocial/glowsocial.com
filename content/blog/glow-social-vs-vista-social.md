## The Core Difference

**Vista Social** is like a professional kitchen — it gives you the best equipment, tools, and workspace to cook amazing meals. But you still have to do the cooking.

**Glow Social** is like a prepared meal kit — the hard part is handled before it reaches you, and you decide what goes on the table.

One isn't better than the other. They're for different people.

## When Vista Social Is the Better Choice

Vista Social is genuinely excellent if you:

<ul>
<li><strong>Have a dedicated social media person or team</strong> — they need professional tools</li>
<li><strong>Manage multiple clients</strong> — Vista Social's agency features (white-label reports, client approval workflows) are purpose-built for this</li>
<li><strong>Need advanced analytics</strong> — competitor benchmarking, cross-platform reports, sentiment analysis</li>
<li><strong>Want social listening</strong> — monitor brand mentions and industry conversations</li>
<li><strong>Manage 15+ social platforms</strong> — Vista Social supports Reddit, Pinterest, YouTube, and more</li>
<li><strong>Need review management</strong> — manage Google, Yelp, and TripAdvisor reviews in one inbox</li>
<li><strong>Invest 5-10+ hours/month in social media</strong> — and want professional tools for that time</li>
</ul>

**Bottom line:** If social media is a core part of your marketing strategy and you have the team to execute it, Vista Social is a top-tier platform at competitive pricing.

## When Glow Social Is the Better Choice

Glow Social is the better choice if you:

<ul>
<li><strong>Don't have a social media person</strong> — you're the owner and you're doing everything</li>
<li><strong>Don't have 5+ hours/month for social media</strong> — you barely have 5 minutes</li>
<li><strong>Just need active pages</strong> — your goal is "look alive online," not "grow a following"</li>
<li><strong>Want total cost under $100/month</strong> — including the time value of content creation</li>
<li><strong>Have been meaning to post but never do</strong> — consistency is the #1 problem</li>
<li><strong>Tried scheduling tools before and stopped using them</strong> — the problem wasn't the tool, it was finding time to create content</li>
</ul>

**Bottom line:** If you know social media matters but you're never going to be the person who creates content, Glow Social gives you posts to approve instead of another dashboard to manage.

Of course, if you enjoy creating content or have a team member doing it, that time cost is already accounted for — and Vista Social gives them excellent tools.

## What About Quality?

Let's be honest:

<ul>
<li><strong>Vista Social + a skilled creator</strong> will produce better, more strategic content than Glow Social's AI</li>
<li><strong>Glow Social's posts</strong> are professional and on-brand, but they won't go viral or feel deeply personal</li>
<li><strong>However:</strong> 20 useful posts ready to approve are infinitely better than 0 posts because you never found time to use Vista Social</li>
</ul>

The best social media tool is the one you actually use. For many small business owners, that's the one that doesn't require any work.

## Can You Switch Later?

Yes. Both tools are month-to-month:

<ul>
<li>Start with Glow Social to establish a consistent presence</li>
<li>When you're ready to invest more time (or hire someone), upgrade to Vista Social for professional management</li>
<li>There's no lock-in, data migration, or switching penalty with either tool</li>
</ul>

## Bottom Line

Vista Social is one of the best social media management platforms available — powerful, well-priced, and well-reviewed. If you have the time and team to use it, it's an excellent choice.

Glow Social exists for a different reality: business owners who need social media handled but don't have the time, skill, or desire to do it themselves.

**Choose Vista Social if:** You want professional tools to manage social media actively.
**Choose Glow Social if:** You want posts ready to approve without managing another social media tool.
