Local businesses often feel pressure to be everywhere—Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube, Pinterest, X. The reality: most local businesses only need 2-3 platforms, and choosing the right ones matters more than being everywhere. Here's how to pick.


## Why You Shouldn't Be on Every Platform

Spreading thin across many platforms leads to:


<ul>
<li>Inconsistent posting (no time to keep up with all of them)</li>
<li>Weak presence on all platforms instead of strong presence on a few</li>
<li>Wasted time learning platforms your customers don't use</li>
<li>Burnout from trying to do too much</li>
</ul>

Better strategy: Pick 2-3 platforms where your customers actually are and focus there.


## The Right Platforms by Business Type

### Restaurants, Cafes, Bars
<strong>Focus on:</strong> Instagram + Google Business Profile

<ul>
<li>Instagram: Food photos, daily specials, behind-the-scenes</li>
<li>Google Business Profile: "Food near me" searches, reviews, hours</li>
<li>Facebook: Good secondary option for events and community</li>
</ul>

### Salons, Spas, Beauty
<strong>Focus on:</strong> Instagram + Facebook + Google Business Profile

<ul>
<li>Instagram: Before/after transformations (essential)</li>
<li>Facebook: Booking, local community, older demographics</li>
<li>TikTok: Optional if you have time for short videos</li>
</ul>

### Contractors, Home Services
<strong>Focus on:</strong> Google Business Profile + Facebook + Nextdoor

<ul>
<li>Google Business Profile: "Plumber near me" drives most leads</li>
<li>Facebook: Local community, project photos</li>
<li>Nextdoor: Neighborhood recommendations are gold</li>
<li>Instagram: Optional for project portfolios</li>
</ul>

### Real Estate Agents
<strong>Focus on:</strong> Instagram + Facebook + LinkedIn

<ul>
<li>Instagram: Property photos, neighborhood content</li>
<li>Facebook: Local community, events, listings</li>
<li>LinkedIn: Professional networking, referral partners</li>
</ul>

### Fitness Studios, Gyms
<strong>Focus on:</strong> Instagram + Facebook

<ul>
<li>Instagram: Classes, energy, transformations</li>
<li>Facebook: Community building, local reach</li>
<li>TikTok: Great if you have time for workout clips</li>
</ul>

### Professional Services (Accountants, Lawyers, Consultants)
<strong>Focus on:</strong> LinkedIn + Google Business Profile

<ul>
<li>LinkedIn: Professional credibility, thought leadership</li>
<li>Google Business Profile: Local search, reviews</li>
<li>Facebook: Optional for local presence</li>
</ul>

### Retail, Boutiques
<strong>Focus on:</strong> Instagram + Facebook

<ul>
<li>Instagram: Product photos, new arrivals</li>
<li>Facebook: Local community, events, sales</li>
<li>Pinterest: Optional for product discovery</li>
</ul>

## How to Decide

Ask these questions:


<strong>1. Where do your customers actually spend time?</strong>

<ul>
<li>Ask current customers directly</li>
<li>Think about their demographics and habits</li>
</ul>

<strong>2. What type of content fits your business?</strong>

<ul>
<li>Visual business (food, beauty, products) → Instagram</li>
<li>Local service business → Facebook + Google Business Profile</li>
<li>Professional services → LinkedIn</li>
</ul>

<strong>3. What can you actually maintain?</strong>

<ul>
<li>2 platforms with consistent posting beats 5 with sporadic activity</li>
<li>Choose platforms you'll actually use</li>
</ul>

## The Minimum Viable Presence

For most local businesses, this is enough:


<ol>
<li><strong>Google Business Profile</strong> (claimed, optimized, reviews collected)</li>
<li><strong>One platform where your customers are</strong> (usually Instagram or Facebook)</li>
<li><strong>Consistent posting</strong> (3x/week minimum)</li>
</ol>

Everything else is optional until you've mastered these basics.
