The worst local business content sounds like marketing.

The best local business content sounds like useful insider knowledge.

That difference showed up clearly in Glow Social's [State of Local Business Social Media 2026](/research/local-business-social-media-statistics). In the dataset, myth-busting posts averaged **5.2% engagement**, and contrarian posts averaged **4.6% engagement**.

Questions and problem-focused posts performed lower.

That should change how local businesses think about content.

## Why Myth-Busting Works

A myth-busting post creates a small pattern interrupt.

Instead of saying, "We offer roof repair," the business says, "Myth: a small ceiling stain always means a small roof problem."

Instead of saying, "Book your facial today," the business says, "Myth: if your skin feels tight after cleansing, that means it is clean."

Instead of saying, "We sell homes in Phoenix," the business says, "Myth: the highest offer is always the best offer."

The customer stops because the post promises a useful correction.

That makes the business sound experienced. It also gives the customer a reason to pay attention without feeling sold to.

## Why Contrarian Posts Work

Contrarian posts are not about being edgy for attention.

They work when a business calmly disagrees with a common assumption in its category.

Examples:

- "You probably do not need a new roof yet."
- "The cheapest social media tool can cost more if you still have to do the work."
- "More followers will not fix an inactive local presence."
- "Daily Instagram posts are not the first thing most local businesses need."
- "A perfect logo matters less than accurate hours, recent posts, and current reviews."

The post works because it feels honest.

Most customers are used to businesses pushing the biggest purchase, the most expensive service, or the flashiest trend. A thoughtful contrarian post builds trust because it shows judgment.

## Why Generic Questions Underperform

For years, small business owners were told to "ask questions for engagement."

That advice created a lot of weak posts:

- "What are your weekend plans?"
- "Coffee or tea?"
- "Who is ready for summer?"
- "What is your favorite color?"

These posts can work for some lifestyle brands with strong communities. For most local service businesses, they feel empty.

Customers do not need their roofer to ask about weekend plans. They need the roofer to help them understand whether the leak is urgent.

## Why Problem Posts Can Feel Like A Setup

Problem-based posts can be useful, but many sound like the opening line of a sales pitch.

For example:

"Are you tired of wasting time on social media?"

That may be true, but customers have seen the structure too many times. The post announces that a pitch is coming.

A stronger version would teach:

"The reason social media feels exhausting for local businesses is that most tools only schedule posts. They do not decide what to say, adapt the post by platform, or keep Google Business Profile current."

That is still about the problem, but it gives the reader a real insight.

## The Best Content Makes The Customer Smarter

If a local business wants better engagement, it should stop asking, "What can we post about ourselves?"

Ask:

- What does the customer misunderstand?
- What advice is common but wrong?
- What mistake costs people money?
- What should buyers know before they call?
- What do insiders notice that customers miss?

Those questions lead to stronger posts because they transfer expertise.

## Examples By Business Type

Here are myth or contrarian angles local businesses can adapt:

- **Dentist:** "Myth: If a tooth stops hurting, the problem is gone."
- **Roofer:** "Myth: You only need to check your roof after visible storm damage."
- **Salon:** "Myth: More heat protectant cancels out daily hot-tool damage."
- **Med spa:** "Contrarian take: More aggressive is not always better for skin treatments."
- **Restaurant:** "Myth: The busiest night is always the best night to visit."
- **Realtor:** "Contrarian take: The highest offer is not always the cleanest offer."
- **Accountant:** "Myth: Tax planning starts in March."
- **Landscaper:** "Myth: More watering fixes every brown lawn."
- **Gym:** "Contrarian take: The best workout plan is the one you can repeat next week."

These posts work because they are specific. They sound like a real professional who has seen the same mistake many times.

## How This Connects To Platform Choice

Myth and contrarian posts can work across platforms, but they behave differently by channel.

- On Facebook, they create useful local trust.
- On Instagram, they can be turned into simple carousels.
- On Google Business Profile, they help buyers make decisions.
- On Pinterest, they can become evergreen education.
- On LinkedIn, they support founder or professional authority.

That is why content category and platform strategy should be connected.

Local businesses do not need more generic content prompts.

Myth-busting and contrarian content work because they are not just content. They are expertise made visible.
