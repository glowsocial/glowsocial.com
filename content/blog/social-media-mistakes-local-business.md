Most small business owners know they should post consistently on social media, but something always gets in the way. The usual pattern: Start strong, get busy with work, stop posting, feel guilty, repeat. If social media isn't working for your business, it's probably one of these common mistakes—and most of them have simple fixes.


## 1. Posting Inconsistently

<strong>The mistake:</strong> Posting every day for two weeks, then disappearing for a month.


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> Social media feels urgent until business gets busy, then it drops to the bottom of the list.


<strong>The fix:</strong> Either batch-create content monthly or use automated posting software. Glow Social publishes 12 posts per month automatically for $49—no matter how busy you get.


## 2. Only Posting When You Need Customers

<strong>The mistake:</strong> Going invisible, then suddenly posting when business is slow.


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> Social media feels like a sales channel rather than a visibility channel.


<strong>The fix:</strong> Post consistently whether you're busy or slow. Social media builds familiarity over time, so when people need what you offer, you're top of mind.


## 3. Making Every Post a Sales Pitch

<strong>The mistake:</strong> "Buy now!" "20% off!" "Book today!" on every single post.


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> You need customers, so you make the ask every time.


<strong>The fix:</strong> Follow the 80/20 rule—80% value (tips, behind-the-scenes, stories) and 20% promotion. People follow you for value, not constant sales pitches.


## 4. Trying to Be on Every Platform

<strong>The mistake:</strong> Spreading thin across Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Twitter, Pinterest, YouTube...


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> "Everyone says you need to be everywhere."


<strong>The fix:</strong> Pick 2-3 platforms where your customers actually spend time. For most local businesses: Google Business Profile + Facebook + Instagram covers it.


## 5. Posting Generic Content

<strong>The mistake:</strong> Sharing random quotes, stock photos, and content that could be from any business.


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> It's faster than creating original content.


<strong>The fix:</strong> Show your actual work, your actual customers (with permission), your actual personality. Generic content doesn't build trust with your specific audience.


## 6. Ignoring Comments and Messages

<strong>The mistake:</strong> Posting content but never responding to engagement.


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> Notifications pile up when you're busy.


<strong>The fix:</strong> Set a daily check—5 minutes to respond to comments and messages. This is the highest-value social media activity you can do.


## 7. No Clear Call to Action

<strong>The mistake:</strong> Posts that don't lead anywhere—no link, no ask, no next step.


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> Fear of being "too salesy" goes too far in the other direction.


<strong>The fix:</strong> Include a soft CTA regularly. "Link in bio," "DM for details," "Visit our website"—make it easy for interested people to take the next step.


## 8. Expecting Instant Results

<strong>The mistake:</strong> Posting for a month, seeing no leads, concluding "social media doesn't work for my business."


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> Misunderstanding how social media works.


<strong>The fix:</strong> Social media is a visibility play, not a direct response channel. It takes 3-6 months of consistency before most businesses see results. Commit to at least 6 months before judging.


## 9. Spending Too Much Time on Content

<strong>The mistake:</strong> 10+ hours per month on social media, taking time from customer work.


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> Perfectionism, overthinking, or inefficient workflow.


<strong>The fix:</strong> Use batch creation (all content in one session) or automated posting. You shouldn't spend more than 2-3 hours per month on social media—or $49/month for Glow Social to handle it automatically.


## 10. Ignoring Google Business Profile

<strong>The mistake:</strong> Focusing on social media while ignoring your Google listing.


<strong>Why it happens:</strong> Google Business Profile isn't "social media" so it gets overlooked.


<strong>The fix:</strong> For local businesses, Google Business Profile often drives more leads than social media. Claim it, optimize it, post updates, and collect reviews. Then worry about social media.


## The Underlying Problem

Most of these mistakes share a root cause: <strong>social media feels like one more thing to do</strong> when you're already overwhelmed running your business.


Two solutions:
