Glow Social costs **$99 per month**.

That includes **20 posts per month** prepared from your business website, image creation, a monthly content calendar, review before publishing, and publishing to connected profiles after you approve the posts. There are no setup fees, no annual contract, and no separate scheduler subscription required for the baseline workflow.

The important pricing question is not only, "Is Glow Social cheaper than Buffer, Later, or Hootsuite?" Those tools mainly help you publish posts you already created. Glow Social is priced for the owner who needs the posts created first.

## Quick Price Answer

| Option | Typical monthly cost | What you still have to do |
|---|---:|---|
| Free or cheap scheduler | $0-$50 | Create every post, caption, graphic, and calendar idea |
| Glow Social | $99 | Review, edit if needed, and approve posts before publishing |
| Basic freelancer | $300-$800 | Onboarding, planning calls, feedback, revisions, vendor management |
| Agency or full-service manager | $1,000+ | Strategy approvals, campaign direction, reporting, more meetings |

Glow Social is the best fit when the job is: **keep the business looking active with posts ready to approve, without hiring a full social media manager.**

## What You Get for $99/Month

The $99/month plan is built around a practical small-business baseline:

- 20 posts per month
- captions written from your business context
- image creation for posts
- a content calendar
- approval before posts go live
- publishing to connected social profiles after approval
- month-to-month pricing
- no setup fee
- no annual contract

That makes the price easier to compare against the real alternatives. If you already love creating content and only need a queue, a scheduler may be enough. If your social pages go quiet because nobody has time to decide what to post, a scheduler is not the thing that removes the bottleneck.

## The Cost Comparison Most Owners Should Use

Many small businesses compare Glow Social with a $6/month scheduling tool and conclude the scheduler is cheaper.

That comparison misses the unpaid work.

A scheduler still leaves you with:

- choosing topics
- writing captions
- creating or finding images
- adapting posts for each platform
- loading the calendar
- remembering to refill the queue
- checking whether the posts fit your business

If that takes even four hours per month, the "cheap" option may cost more in owner time than the subscription price suggests. ## When Glow Social Is Worth the Price

Glow Social is usually worth considering when:

- your Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, or Google Business Profile pages look quiet
- you want customers to see recent activity when they check you out
- you do not have a reliable person creating posts every week
- your budget is closer to $100/month than $500-$1,500/month
- you want to review posts before anything publishes
- you need a baseline, not a full marketing department

The strongest fit is a local or service business with a useful website, clear services, and enough proof or business context for posts to be created from existing material.

## When Glow Social Is Not the Right Spend

Glow Social is not meant to replace every social media job.

Hire a freelancer, manager, or agency instead if you need:

- daily comment and DM replies
- custom Reels or on-site video shoots
- influencer campaigns
- paid social ad management
- deep brand strategy
- multi-location reporting
- someone to make judgment calls without owner review

For that decision, compare [social media manager costs](/blog/freelance-social-media-manager-charge-cost), the broader [social media management pricing guide](/blog/social-media-management-cost-pricing-guide), and [whether hiring a social media manager is worth it](/blog/is-hiring-a-social-media-manager-worth-it-for-small-business).

## Real-World Example

A solo financial advisor was weighing three options: a freelancer at $450 per month for 8 posts, Hootsuite at roughly the same monthly budget once the right plan was selected, or Glow Social at $99 per month with content creation included.

The freelancer produced good work but required onboarding, planning, and revision rounds. The scheduler gave the advisor a calendar, but not the posts to put in it. Glow Social was the better starting point because the advisor mainly needed compliant, educational post drafts ready to review from existing website material.

That is the pricing gap Glow Social is designed for: more useful than an empty scheduler, lighter and cheaper than hiring a person before you know you need one.

## Bottom Line

Glow Social costs **$99/month for 20 posts ready to approve**.

Choose it if your main problem is getting a consistent set of posts created and published after review. Choose a scheduler if your posts already exist. Choose a freelancer or agency if social media needs human strategy, custom creative, customer replies, ads, or campaign ownership.
