Local businesses have two digital storefronts that matter most: their **Google Business Profile** (the listing that appears when someone Googles your business) and their **social media pages** (where people go to see if you're active and legitimate).

Most local businesses neglect one or both. Here's why you need both — and how to manage them without hiring anyone.

## What Each Does (They're Complementary, Not Competitive)

<table>
<tr><th>Function</th><th>Google Business Profile</th><th>Social Media</th></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Primary purpose</strong></td><td>Get found by new customers</td><td>Build trust and stay top-of-mind</td></tr>
<tr><td>Shows up in</td><td>Google Search, Google Maps</td><td>Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok</td></tr>
<tr><td>Drives</td><td>Phone calls, directions, website visits</td><td>Brand awareness, referrals, trust</td></tr>
<tr><td>Best for</td><td>People actively searching for your service</td><td>People checking if you're legitimate</td></tr>
<tr><td>Content type</td><td>Business info, photos, reviews, posts</td><td>Regular posts, updates, behind-the-scenes</td></tr>
<tr><td>Customer stage</td><td>Ready to buy (high intent)</td><td>Considering options (mid intent)</td></tr>
</table>

**The one-two punch:** GBP gets customers to your door. Social media convinces them to walk through it.

## The Customer Journey (How They Work Together)

<ol>
<li><strong>Customer searches</strong> "dentist near me" or "best Italian restaurant Scottsdale"</li>
<li><strong>Your GBP appears</strong> in the local pack with reviews, hours, and photos</li>
<li><strong>Customer clicks through</strong> and checks your website and social media</li>
<li><strong>Active social media</strong> confirms you're real, current, and professional</li>
<li><strong>Empty social media</strong> creates doubt — "are they still open?"</li>
<li><strong>Customer decides</strong> to call, book, or visit based on the complete picture</li>
</ol>

You can have the best Google listing in your area, but if your Instagram hasn't posted in 6 months, you're losing customers at step 5.

## The Minimum Viable Setup

### Google Business Profile (1-2 hours/month)

<ul>
<li><strong>Complete your profile 100%</strong> — every field filled, 10+ photos, services listed, hours accurate</li>
<li><strong>Respond to every review</strong> within 24 hours (even 1-2 sentences is fine)</li>
<li><strong>Post Google Updates</strong> weekly (can be the same content as social media)</li>
<li><strong>Add new photos</strong> monthly (phone photos are fine)</li>
<li><strong>Update hours</strong> for holidays and special events</li>
</ul>
