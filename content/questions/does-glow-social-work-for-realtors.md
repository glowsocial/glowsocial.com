# Does Glow Social work for realtors?

Yes. Glow Social generates real estate-specific content including market updates, neighborhood spotlights, home buying and selling tips, staging advice, and client milestone posts. The platform creates content matched to your local market, specialties, and personal brand.

## Why This Matters
Real estate is one of the most competitive industries on social media. Every agent in your market is posting, and the ones who show up consistently win the listing appointments. The challenge is that realtors are constantly on the road: showing houses, attending closings, negotiating deals, and prospecting. Content creation falls to Sunday nights or gets skipped entirely. done-for-you content keeps the pipeline visible even when you're too busy closing deals to write a caption. For realtors specifically, the content needs to be hyperlocal (your city, your neighborhoods, your market conditions) and authoritative (market data, process explanations, buyer/seller education). Generic "5 tips for home buyers" posts from a national template don't build local authority.

## Real-World Example
A solo realtor covering three suburban neighborhoods was spending 6 hours every Sunday batching content for the week: market stats from MLS, neighborhood photos, and listing announcements. After switching to Glow Social, she got 20 posts per month covering mortgage rate commentary, seasonal moving tips, local school district highlights, and first-time buyer guidance. Her Sunday sessions dropped to 15 minutes of reviewing and approving. Her LinkedIn engagement (where she generates most referrals) increased by 55% because she went from 2 posts per week to 3 without any additional time investment.

## What Most People Get Wrong
Realtors assume social media content needs to be listing-focused. Listings are important, but they only matter to the tiny fraction of followers actively buying or selling right now. The 95% of your audience not in a transaction still needs to see you consistently so they think of you when their time comes. Educational and community content builds that long-term pipeline.
