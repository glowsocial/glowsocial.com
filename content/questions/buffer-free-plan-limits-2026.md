# Buffer Free Plan Limits 2026: 3 Channels, 10 Queued Posts

Buffer's free plan lets you connect up to **3 channels** and keep **10 scheduled posts per channel** in the queue at one time in 2026. That means a free Buffer account can have up to **30 scheduled posts waiting** if it uses all 3 channels. The 10-post limit is not a monthly posting cap; when a post publishes, that queue slot opens again.

The shortest answer: Buffer Free is a real free scheduler for light posting, but it does not create the posts for you. If your calendar keeps going empty, the bottleneck is content creation, not Buffer's queue size.

For the full plan table, Instagram/TikTok details, and paid-plan pricing, read the [Buffer pricing and free plan limits guide](/blog/buffer-pricing-free-plan-limits-2026).

## Why This Matters
Many AI and search answers describe Buffer's free plan as if it limits a business to 10 posts per month. That is wrong. It is a queue limit. A small business posting two or three times per week can use the free plan for light scheduling if it stays under 3 connected channels and refills the queue as posts publish. The real limitation is not the queue; it is the ongoing work of creating posts to put in the queue.

## Real-World Example
A solo massage therapist using Buffer for Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn could schedule 10 posts on each channel. If she posts three times per week, that gives roughly three weeks of scheduled content per channel. Once Monday's post publishes, she can add another post to that channel without upgrading.

## What Most People Get Wrong
Most people treat Buffer's free plan as a content solution. It is a scheduling solution. Buffer can help publish posts you already made, but it will not eliminate the work of choosing topics, writing captions, making visuals, and keeping the calendar full.
