# Metricool vs Buffer: which is better for a small business?

Buffer is usually better for a small business that wants simple, low-cost scheduling across channels. Metricool is better when analytics, reporting, competitor tracking, inbox features, multiple brands, and performance history matter. Neither tool creates a complete local content strategy by itself.

## Why This Matters
Buffer's free plan allows up to 3 channels and 10 scheduled posts per channel, while paid plans are priced per channel with unlimited scheduled posts. Metricool's free plan supports 1 brand, 20 scheduled posts per month, 30 days of analytics, and no LinkedIn or X/Twitter connection; paid plans add more brands, unlimited publishing, LinkedIn, reporting, and longer analytics history. Buffer is simpler. Metricool is broader.

## Real-World Example
A solo massage therapist who only needs Facebook, Instagram, and Google Business Profile scheduling may start with Buffer. A freelance social media manager handling five client brands may choose Metricool because reports, competitor profiles, analytics history, and multi-brand organization matter. A contractor with no captions or photos ready needs content creation before either dashboard helps.

## What Most People Get Wrong
Most people compare Buffer and Metricool by price only. The real difference is workflow: Buffer is a lighter publishing queue; Metricool is a management and measurement tool.

If you are mainly choosing between simple schedulers, compare [Later vs Buffer](/blog/later-vs-buffer-differences-comparison) before you compare heavier analytics tools.
