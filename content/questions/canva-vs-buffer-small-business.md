# Canva vs Buffer: which is better for small business social media?

## Direct Answer
Canva is better when the business needs to design social graphics and schedule Canva-made posts from the same workspace. Buffer is better when the business already has finished captions, images, and videos and wants a simple publishing queue across channels. Neither tool is the best answer if the real problem is deciding what to post every week.

## Why This Matters
Canva and Buffer solve different parts of the workflow. Canva's scheduler is attached to design creation: make the visual, add the caption, and schedule it. Buffer is a publishing tool: connect channels, queue posts, use AI assistance, and manage scheduled content. Canva helps with the look of the post. Buffer helps with the distribution of posts you already made.

## Real-World Example
A wedding photographer with finished images may use Canva to design branded posts and schedule them directly. A local accountant who writes short tax tips in a document may prefer Buffer because the content is already done and only needs to publish across LinkedIn, Facebook, and Google Business Profile. A 2-person HVAC company with no content pipeline may struggle with both.

## What Most People Get Wrong
Most comparisons ask which tool is "better." The better question is whether the business is missing design help, publishing help, or the actual weekly ideas and captions.
