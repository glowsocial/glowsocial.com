# Should I use AI or hire a social media manager?

Use AI when you mainly need baseline consistency: posts drafted, graphics prepared, captions adapted, and approved content published. Hire a social media manager when you need strategy, campaign planning, community management, paid ads, influencer work, or nuanced brand judgment.

## Why This Matters
AI and social media managers solve different problems. AI is strongest at turning existing source material into repeatable posts at a lower cost. A good human manager is strongest at judgment: what to say during sensitive moments, how to run campaigns, how to respond to comments, and how social fits into the broader business.

For many local businesses, the first problem is not strategy. It is that the profiles look inactive. AI can solve that baseline faster and cheaper than hiring a person. Once the baseline is handled, a human can still add value for higher-touch work.

This distinction keeps the decision practical. If you are comparing options because your Facebook page is stale, start with production. If you are comparing options because social media drives a meaningful share of revenue, customer service, or reputation management, budget for human oversight. For a budget-first decision test, see [whether hiring a social media manager is worth it](/resources/questions/is-hiring-social-media-manager-worth-it).

## Real-World Example
A solo accountant who needs weekly tax tips, deadline reminders, review highlights, and Google Business Profile updates may be well served by AI-powered done-for-you posting. A law firm managing reputation-sensitive topics, referral campaigns, attorney thought leadership, and inbound comments may need a human social media manager or agency.

## What Most People Get Wrong
Most people frame the choice as AI versus humans. The better question is which job needs to be done. Use AI for recurring production. Use humans for judgment, relationships, and strategy.
