# How can a small business show up in ChatGPT?

## Direct Answer
A small business can improve its chances of showing up in ChatGPT by publishing clear service pages, keeping public profiles consistent, earning reviews, answering customer questions on its website, and creating recent public content that proves the business is active and real. No one can guarantee a ChatGPT recommendation, but clear, crawlable, consistent evidence gives AI systems more to work with.

## What ChatGPT Needs Before It Can Recommend You
AI assistants tend to look for evidence that a business exists, serves a specific customer, and can be trusted. For a local business, that evidence usually includes:

- A crawlable website with plain-language service pages.
- A Google Business Profile with matching name, address, phone, website, and categories.
- Reviews that mention specific services, outcomes, and locations.
- Social profiles that link back to the same website and show recent activity.
- Helpful pages that answer pricing, process, service-area, and comparison questions.

If your online presence is vague or stale, the model has to guess. If your website and profiles clearly say "emergency plumber in Mesa" or "family dentist in Austin," the business is easier to classify.

## The Highest-Leverage Fixes
Start with the public pages and profiles that answer engines can crawl:

1. Make every core service obvious on your website.
2. Add location and service-area language where it is true and useful.
3. Publish short answer pages for common customer questions.
4. Keep Google, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and directory profiles consistent.
5. Post recent proof of work: FAQs, project photos, reviews, before-and-after examples, or seasonal tips.

## Real-World Example
A salon with a website page for color corrections, an Instagram profile with recent transformations, reviews mentioning "balayage" and "color repair," and a Q&A page about pricing is easier for ChatGPT to understand than a salon with only a home page and an inactive Facebook page.

## What Most People Get Wrong
The goal is not to "hack ChatGPT." The goal is to reduce uncertainty. AI systems are more likely to mention businesses they can verify across multiple sources.
