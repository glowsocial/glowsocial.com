# Should a local business focus on Google Business Profile, Instagram, or Facebook?

A local business should usually focus on Google Business Profile first, then Facebook and Instagram. Google Business Profile captures high-intent search and Maps visibility. Facebook and Instagram help customers confirm the business is active, trustworthy, and current before they call, book, or visit.

## Why This Matters
Google Business Profile shows up when someone is already searching for a service, direction, phone number, hours, reviews, or nearby option. That intent is hard to beat. Social media works differently. Facebook and Instagram build recognition, show recent proof, and answer the quiet question customers often have: "Is this business still active and worth trusting?"

## Real-World Example
A solo plumber should first claim and complete Google Business Profile, add services, keep hours accurate, collect reviews, upload photos, and post useful updates. Then the plumber should keep Facebook and Instagram alive with job photos, maintenance tips, review highlights, and seasonal reminders. The goal is not to become an influencer. The goal is to look current wherever customers check.

## What Most People Get Wrong
The mistake is treating GBP and social media as competitors. For local businesses, GBP usually drives discovery, while Facebook and Instagram support trust after discovery.
