# What should a roofer post on social media?

A roofer should post completed projects, storm-prep tips, leak warning signs, inspection reminders, customer reviews, service-area updates, warranty or financing notes, and seasonal roof maintenance advice. Roofing content should reduce risk and help homeowners know when to request an estimate.

## Best Post Categories For Roofers
Useful roofing content answers high-stakes homeowner questions:

- Do I need an inspection after a storm?
- What are signs of roof damage?
- How long does replacement take?
- What does a finished project look like?
- Are other local homeowners happy with this company?
- What areas does the roofer serve?

## A Simple Monthly Mix
Use this 12-post starter calendar:

1. Three completed project posts.
2. Two storm or weather-prep posts.
3. Two leak or inspection education posts.
4. Two review posts.
5. Two service-area posts.
6. One estimate or booking reminder.

## Real-World Example
A post that shows a recent replacement in a nearby neighborhood with a short explanation of the problem and result builds more trust than a generic "call us today" graphic.

## What Most People Get Wrong
Roofing content does not need to be flashy. It needs to make the homeowner feel safer choosing you.
